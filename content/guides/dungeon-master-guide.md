# DUNGEON MASTER GUIDE

Crossroads DMs are worldbuilders and facilitators. The goal is to create opportunities for player stories, not to replace them.

## DM Event Philosophy

- Create pressure, mystery, and consequence.
- Reward initiative and collaboration.
- Keep outcomes coherent with setting and prior events.
- Let player choices materially affect future hooks.

## Event Types

### Crossroads DM Events

- Staff-run events tied to active plots, factions, and world state.
- Can be small-party or larger server-impact sessions.

### Player-Run Events

- Player-initiated story events with optional DM support.
- Strongly encouraged when coordinated through tickets and clear intent.

### Dungeons and Builds

- Encounter spaces should support meaningful decision points.
- Build and pacing should prioritize readability and fair challenge.

## Running Better Events

- Pre-brief stakes and tone.
- Keep objective clarity high.
- Track consequences and follow up in world state.
- Provide post-event hooks for non-attending players to engage.

## Ticketing and Coordination

- Use tickets for planning support, approvals, and follow-up.
- Record outcomes and unresolved threads after each event.
- Keep communication concise and actionable.

## Player Expectations

- Engage with hooks, do not wait passively.
- Coordinate with others to create shared momentum.
- Bring character-driven goals into event participation.
