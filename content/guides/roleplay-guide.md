# ROLEPLAY GUIDE

This guide sets baseline etiquette for consistent, collaborative roleplay on Crossroads.

## Core Standard

Roleplay is collaborative storytelling, not a competition.  
When in doubt, choose the action that improves the scene for everyone involved.

## Scene Etiquette

- Entering scenes: observe a post or two so your entry fits tone and pacing.
- Exiting scenes: leave space for responses before hard exiting.
- OOC travel commands (`/warp`, `/home`, etc.): do not use mid-scene without consent.
- Avoid rapid out-of-order posting in crowded scenes.
- Keep post quality readable and actionable.

## Play-To-Lift Practices

- Give others clear hooks to respond to.
- Accept setbacks and consequences when narratively appropriate.
- Share spotlight; do not monopolize progression scenes.
- Use conflict to build story, not to shut others down.

## Communication

- Clarify OOC intent when scenes are tense or ambiguous.
- Use respectful tone in all disputes.
- Move disagreements to tickets if needed, not public escalation.

## Identifying Races and Knowledge Boundaries

- Treat rare race recognition as contextual, not automatic.
- Separate player knowledge from character knowledge.
- Ask for clarification in-character when uncertain.

## Consent and Boundaries

- Respect roleplay boundaries and server rules at all times.
- Do not force outcomes that require consent without following rules systems.
- If a scene becomes unclear, pause and align OOC before continuing.
