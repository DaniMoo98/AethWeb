# CONSEQUENCE RULES

> All rules are subject to change and will be posted and documented in the Discord until the website can be updated. Thank you for your consideration. ~Staff

The best development comes from various types of failure. It doesn't matter if the failing is a task gone wrong, missing your mark, or conflict with another character — the experience will end in a drawback for your character. These drawbacks are considered consequences which start a road towards further developing your character.

This page will guide you through not only PvP conflict but also any type of natural consequence your character may face.

---

## i. IC Choices & IC Consequences

It is recommended to think through each situation for the best way to proceed. Your character's actions will result in consequences. Whether good or bad, consequences that come from an IC choice are to be played out.

## ii. Player Stories

Each consequence needs to appropriately match the error made by the character and applied so that story can continue for every player involved. Consequences should **not** be given with the goal of ending someone's story or being the winner.

- Communication is paramount. The player is required to send a direct message communicating with the other party, especially if consequences are involved after a PvP encounter. Lack of clear communication will result in conflict both in and out of character.

## iii. Respect Consent

Not all players are comfortable with certain heavy scenarios such as torture, dismemberment, and graphic or sexual content. Any roleplay of this nature needs to be discussed out of character before the roleplay occurs and consent given.

- **Mutual consent overrides guidelines.** If both the player giving and receiving consequences agree to consequences that would normally be restricted, they may apply more than we would otherwise allow.
- Players should decide what cons they are comfortable with **before** bestowing any.
- Players may not unilaterally take mechanical cons like wounds without gaining consent from all combat parties.

### FADE TO BLACK

Fade-To-Black (FTB) is when a victim requests to forgo the roleplay of the consequence allotted. All FTB requests are **required to be honored** by the aggressor. Attempting to coerce or force a player to roleplay out certain scenarios will result in severe disciplinary action.

### FADE TO BLACK CONSENT ABUSE

Players are **not** allowed to abuse consent rules or FTB to bypass consequences. The agreed upon consequences **DO** happen, whether or not the roleplay is played out. Anyone who abuses these rules will receive disciplinary action.

## iv. Consequence Options

Consequences don't always need to lead to dramatic endings. Below are the options:

### Regulated Consequences

These kinds of cons **cannot be stacked without consent**:

- Mechanical (RPR) wounds to the allowed ratio
- Serious injury
- Theft (see Theft Rules)
- Persistent magical effects like Bestow Curse
- Magically compelled interrogation
- Physical torture to interrogate
- Capture
- Collecting ritual material (blood, hair, etc.)
  - a. Material is carried by the person who took it, or transferred via RP
  - b. Transfers must be logged via screenshots
  - c. The user of the material may request the thief be added to their ticket for verification
  - d. Only one ritual worth of material can be taken per combat con; consumed on use
  - e. If the person cannot produce a log, they do not get the bonus
- Limb Removal
  - a. Not as a first encounter, with exceptions for very poor behavior or with consent
  - b. Not available if your side outnumbers the other by 2 or more people (unless specifically requested)

### Non-Regulated Consequences

These types of consequences **can be stacked** (consent is polite but not required):

- Unmasking (suggest reserving for a second encounter)
- Nonmagical interrogation without wounds (threats, bribery, blackmail)
- Name calling/insulting
- Physical discomfort without injury (pinning, temporary restraint)
- Theft of fluff items (earring, bracelet, handkerchief, etc.) with appropriate OOC communication

### Caveats

Cons may escalate depending on behavior history. First encounters with significant escalation or NVL may warrant more serious cons than usual.

**Use of Body Parts Taken as Consequences:**
1. Body parts or bodies must be indicated with a POI when moved to a new location, including ritual status
2. If a player rerolls while in possession of a body part, they may pass it to another character or bring it with them (in which case it is considered destroyed for resurrection)
3. Corpses can be located via Locate Object but cannot be scried
4. Bodies/body parts not preserved by Gentle Repose are considered "destroyed" after 30 IRL days
5. Bodies and body parts may not be stored in demiplanes
6. Player bodies may not be reanimated into undead

### CAPTIVE RP

The captor is required to provide RP for the captive. If **30 minutes** go by without any RP from the captor, the captive is allowed to leave the scene.

### LEFT FOR DEAD

Left for Dead (LFD) allows characters to disengage from conflict without permanently ending their story. The winner believes the other character is dead. The downed character, not actually dead, can continue their story — but must be careful given that the winner thinks they are dead. Being LFD does not reduce the severity of consequences if encountered again.

## v. PLAYER DEATH

Permanent Death should be proceeded with caution and as a last resort. A ticket is required to request the ability to potentially kill another player's character. A well thought out reason and proof of interactions must be provided. Not all requests will be approved.

### DM Events & Death

DMNPC's killing a player character will not be permissible during a DM event without consent. PVP kills are allowed if communicated with all parties. **In the event of NVL behavior, an LFD consequence may be issued and may be escalated to player death upon staff review.**

### Death Appeal

If your character's death was accepted but you feel it was not justified, you may appeal via ticket with evidence.

### Death as Consequence

Once your PK ticket has been approved, RP must take place with screenshots provided. Approval does **NOT** mean the character is automatically killed — RP must occur.

## vi. PK RULES

We encourage players to be creative with consequences and seek PK as a **last resort**.

### Scene Lock

Once PvP starts, the scene is locked. The **4 Emote Rule** applies after combat concludes — 4 rounds of emotes occur before the scene is unlocked. This time can be used to inflict consequences or relocate a downed character.

### Consent

Defendant's consent overrules all PK rules. If a player would prefer to reroll, the PK will be approved with a required ticket.

### Voluntary Death

The defender can voluntarily PK their character, forgoing the PK request process.

### Mutual Escalation

Both parties must be antagonizing each other. Proof of mutual escalation showing circumstances enough to end a character's life is required for PK approval.

### No Value of Life (NVL)

When a character acts recklessly in a way that does not make sense for their situation. Examples include a monstrous creature walking into a populated town without stealth, or a tortured character showing no fear or pain. A PK ticket can be created for staff review.

### Extreme Circumstance

Certain extreme situations — such as torture and complete destruction of a character's life — may warrant PK without mutual escalation. A moderation ticket with proof is required.

### Requirements

Players must submit no less than **three (3) fully documented incidents** of roleplay where the characters are at odds, as well as three incidents of RP demonstrating adequate story between the players.

### Expiration

Approved PKs remain in play for **three months**. After this, players may reapply with continued proof.

### Shelf/Unshelf Consideration

Staff may provide a settling-in period after unshelving. There is a minimum **14-day period** after creating/unshelving a character where any previously approved PK will be paused.

## vii. NON-CONSENSUAL SEXUAL INTERACTIONS AS PVP CONSEQUENCE

Non-consensual sexual consequences as a result of a PVP down are **strictly prohibited** at Aethrya. If you or someone you know has experienced this, you may open a ticket.

## viii. NON-CONSENSUAL SEXUAL INTERACTIONS OUTSIDE OF COMBAT

While Aethrya does not prohibit all situations of this nature, **consent is the pillar of any consequence**:

- **All parties** involved must give prior consent, which can be revoked at any time
- Unwilling witnesses must have the scene moved away from them
- FTB requests must be honored
- Parties engaging in such RP cannot subject uninvolved parties to that narrative without consent

---

## ix. SKIRMISH CONSEQUENCES

Skirmish consequences result from fights with **Unfavorable Odds** (winners outnumber victims by 2+: 3v1, 4v2, 5v3, etc.). These are small, ICly insignificant consequences.

Players who **/PVP Observe** count towards the ratio for calculating cons.

**Examples include:**
- Cuts & Bruises (Lesser Wounds)
- Interrogation
- Petty Theft (see Skirmish: Theft)
- Curse with OOC Consent
- Intimidation
- Threatening further action
- Concussion
- Collecting ritual material (same rules as regulated cons)

## x. SKIRMISH CONSEQUENCES: THEFT

Skirmish theft is limited compared to standard theft. You may only take **15 Currency** (as opposed to 30). You may only enact this consequence as opposed to another, and cannot stack consequences with this option.
