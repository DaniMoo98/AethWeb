# HUB CITIES & HUB GUARD RULES

Hub Guards are the unnamed backbone of protection in any faction-run hub city. Conflicts that happen within hub cities do not go unnoticed for very long before guards intervene. These rules do **NOT** apply to player-clan settlements, or clan settlements allied with factions.

---

## Hub Guard Rules

**Following 5 turns of combat**, NPC city guards will enter a scene and apprehend the offender(s). An 'instant defeat' occurs and with the next round of emotes, the offender(s) are taken into custody. The players involved will discuss how to move forward and decide on consequences (if any) to be given.

If the offender(s) are able to **flee before the end of turn 5** in combat, they are not apprehended and are free to continue fleeing from the scene. The offender(s) must leave the city immediately following their exit emote. If they do not leave the city within at maximum 2 rounds of emotes or 10 minutes of fleeing, they may be engaged by another player. Combat and noted emote phases are locked scenes, as ruled on the Combat Rules page. All passerby must continue on their way or arrive after the combat/flee is over.

> This does not exclude them from possible IC consequences from their actions, including but not limited to: warrants for arrest, bounties, and IC bans from the hub city.

All players are expected to use proper roleplay etiquette and conduct combat as normal, following all appropriate combat and flee rules. Any suspected abuse of the Hub Guard system — such as goading to pick fights or inappropriately drawing out fights to bring in the NPC guards — should be reported via a ticket.

### Priority Zones

Factions may designate **two areas** as priority zones: a political building and a military building. These areas have a *heightened guard presence* compared to the rest of the hub. This area covers the **immediate entrance** to the building and the **interior** — it does not cover the exterior.

When in a priority zone, hub guard rules come into effect at the **end of the third round** of combat, rather than the end of the fifth round. This reflects the heightened security in the area. *These areas must be marked with a POI.*

This rule is for small, spontaneous, non-raid/siege conflicts only. Examples include a fight broken out at the tavern, a heated argument in the street, or someone attempting to steal from the marketplace.
