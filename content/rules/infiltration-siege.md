# INFILTRATION, RAID, & SIEGE CONFLICT RULES

---

## I. INFILTRATION

##### Solo or Small Group Incursions Into Another Clan's Territory or Base

Infiltration is the act of 1–3 characters trespassing into another clan or player's build. This interaction is not always planned ahead, but an infiltration ticket **must** be opened **before entry** and include your intentions — such as theft, vandalism, information gathering, pranks, or other shenanigans.

If a build is public (such as a player-run tavern) but your intentions within that building are nefarious or malicious in nature, an infiltration ticket should still be opened.

### Infiltration Cooldowns

Infiltrations have a cooldown clan to clan, determined by type:

- **No mechanical item theft** (may steal letters/fluff items): **3-day** cooldown
- **Mechanical RPR item theft** (crafting materials, armor, weapons, consumables): **7-day** cooldown
- **PVP-interrupted infiltrations** still receive a cooldown (3 or 7 days based on theft) and may not continue after the fight

Screenshots should be taken of your entire inventory before starting and included in your ticket. You do **not** need to wait for staff approval to proceed once the ticket is open.

> **However, the target must have at least one player in their clan online.** Using lockpick or breach when a clan is offline will automatically fail.

### Required Screenshots (uncropped)

- Clan name
- Pre-infiltration & post-infiltration inventory
- Point of entry & exit locations (POI), and any associated rolls
- Stealth rolls and other relevant rolls
- Any uses of Breach (list name of gate/door)
- Any uses of Lockpicking (list name of door or chest/object)
- Any climbing acts (over 4 tiles = DC 18 Athletics/Acrobatics check)
- Any mechanical theft per theft rules
- Any POIs placed for vandalism, disturbances, or other interactions

*Failure to provide screenshots can result in return of stolen goods and possible disciplinary action.*

**Copy-paste the infiltration ticket number into any POI that results from said infiltration.**

### Aquatic Base Infiltrations

Infiltrations to partially or fully submerged bases require proof of water breathing in the ticket:
- Aquatic Perk via race or class
- Potion of Water Breathing
- Water Breathing Ritual proof of cast

> The Waterborn Warrior feat does not provide full water breathing benefits — a secondary form of proof is required.

### Points of Entry

A specific point of entry must be designated in a POI and screenshotted:

- **Gates** — may ONLY be breached (not lockpicked, even if the button lights up)
- **Doors** — may be breached and lockpicked, including double doors
- **Walls** — may be climbed, flown over, swam under/around. Climbing 4+ foundations high requires a **DC 18** Athletics or Acrobatics check. On failure, you fall and take 1 rank of wounds. You may re-attempt at your own risk.
- **Teleportation** — Spells like Misty Step or Dimension Door may be used if the area can be accessed without breaking doors. If teleporting into a locked room, staff assistance and scheduling is needed.
- **Coercive/Alternative Entry** — Forcing others to open doors still requires a ticket and all infiltration POI requirements.

### Small Group Combat in Player Bases

If 2–3 players enter a clan's base with premeditated combat intent, an infiltration ticket is required. If **4 or more**, a raid ticket is required.

Spontaneous combat (3 or fewer intruders) does not require a pre-opened ticket but must be logged after the fight with all standard infiltration documentation.

## II. HUB INFILTRATION RULES

Faction Infiltration differs from standard infiltrations in one primary respect: the presence of **High Security Zones** within the Faction Hub.

### Residential Zones

- Entry requires a **Stealth Check against DC 15**
- Failure results in guard discovery and **Lesser Wounds**
- Theft is limited to a cumulative value of **6** per building, contributing to the hub maximum of **9**

### High Security Zones

- Each Faction has **2–3 High Security Zones** positioned at strategically important areas
- Guard Posts function as blocks requiring Stealth Checks
- The DC is determined by the **Level of Alertness**

### Stealth Checks in High Security Zones

- Failure results in immediate expulsion, Lesser Wounds, and disallowance from entering any other High Security Zone during that attempt
- All rolls must be screenshot and included in the ticket

### Level of Alertness

The alertness level affects DCs in High Security Zones:

| Tier | DC | Condition |
|------|-----|-----------|
| 0 (Default) | 15 | Normal |
| 1 | 20 | After 1 infiltration |
| 2 | 25 | After 2 infiltrations |
| 3 (Max) | 30 | After 3 infiltrations |

The Level of Alertness decreases by **one tier per week**, tracked from the most recent infiltration.

## III. RAIDS

##### Clan Versus Clan

Raids consist of a large group of **4+ players** (up to 8v8) arriving at another clan's base with intent to break in or fight. These are typically **not** stealth-based operations. A **raid ticket must be submitted BEFORE trespassing**.

- If a base lacks walls, moving into the boundaries of the outermost structures qualifies as entering the base
- It is highly encouraged to open a raid ticket pre-emptively if tensions are rising

### Consequences

Determined **BEFORE** the battle, within the raid ticket:
- **One consequence per player** involved
- **One consequence for the clan** as a whole (typically the narrative reason)

Examples include:
- Capturing/rescuing characters
- Stealing resources/equipment up to a tier value of 6
- Destruction of property within reason

**A staff member will be invisible and present at the raid to observe.**

### Build Destruction

It is up to the **defenders** how they visually represent damage. This prevents accidental deletion of chests and materials. **Communication is key!**

> If a group of 4+ enters combat on another clan's base, a raid ticket MUST be created. If negotiations turn to combat, the scene MUST pause and a raid ticket opened.

## IV. SIEGES

##### Faction Versus Faction

Sieges consist of one faction sieging another, up to **10v10**. A **Siege ticket MUST be opened BEFORE** any full plans are created. A DM team member will assist both parties.

### Consequences

Same structure as raids — determined **BEFORE** the battle:
- One consequence per player
- One consequence for the faction as a whole

The coordinating DM and a moderator will monitor the fight. One staff member monitors the attackers, the other monitors the defenders.

### Build Destruction

Same rules as raids — it is up to the **defenders** how they visually represent structural damage but property destruction is expected to happen during a siege.

**Communication is key! Don't forget to communicate with your opponents and staff members on what is and what is not acceptable.**
