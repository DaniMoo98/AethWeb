# PRACTICAL DISGUISE RULES

Practical disguises are simple disguises used to hide one's identity without major distortion or change of character imagery.

---

## What Practical Disguises Include

These disguises should include things such as:

- Light makeup (blush, face paint, lipstick)
- Body-covering attire (hoods, masks, long sleeves, etc.)
- Wigs and hair color changes
- Items that can be realistically used to cover racial features

## What Should NOT Be Used

The following should **not** be used with practical disguises:

- Full-coverage body paints to alter skin tone
- Colored contacts
- Any other alterations to permanent features of your body
- Hoods that would sit oddly over larger horns instead of obscuring them

Use logical judgement of how cloth would physically interact with your character's horns before attempting to obscure them using a hood, or consider a different method like a poofy-haired wig.

---

## Practical Disguise Rules

Practical disguises may use vague titles to act as aliases (Hooded Fiend, Shadowed Hunter, etc.). However, if you are using a name for your disguise, it will need to be **registered through a ticket** to avoid accidental impersonation of another active player.

- Changing very minor features such as hair color or face paint should allow for people with previous interactions with the character to be able to roll Insight to identify your disguise.
- Recognizing a fully disguised person based on voice is **not** permitted on Aethrya.
- Impersonation of other players (excluding the Actor feat) is **not** permitted on Aethrya. This may not be done in ToT, name, or with notice boards.
- Tails can be hidden beneath skirts/pants, so long as they offer logical space.

> **Example:** Skin-tight pants would not have the possibility to hide a full tail.
