# THEFT RULES

All characters with intentions to engage in any form of thievery must strictly adhere to the regulations outlined on this page. Failure to observe these guidelines will result in retrospective alterations and/or the restoration of pilfered item(s) to their rightful owners.

---

## STEALTH OVERVIEW

A character who plans to approach players without being noticed must make a **Stealth roll** when they are within chat range (**16 tiles**) of the nearest player to establish their Stealth Difficulty Class (DC). If the nearest player can see the character attempting stealth, they may make a **Perception check**. If they surpass the DC, the hidden character is revealed.

- The **responsibility** of making the Stealth roll lies with the character trying to hide, not the players being observed
- The player must roll Stealth **FIRST**, before being spotted
- If a player neglects to roll Stealth when they are meant to be concealed, they can be approached without the need for a roll

**Perception checks based on hearing** can be made within a range of **8 tiles**. If the stealthed character moves within 8 tiles of another player, that player can make a Perception check against the established Stealth DC.

> **Note:** Remaining undetected requires the use of concealment. Examples include: positioned behind a structure, hidden among foliage, obscured by a geological formation while crouched, or concealed in dim lighting while wearing dark or camouflage attire.

---

## ESPIONAGE & SPYING

To surreptitiously listen in on a conversation or interaction, a character must approach the scene after successfully achieving the necessary criteria to enter stealth.

- The concealed character is only expected to emote when it is appropriate — such as when they come within view or interact with objects in the environment
- When a player wishes to listen in, they must convincingly express their attempt to actively listen
- If the conversation participants become aware someone is eavesdropping, they may perform a **Perception check** to detect the attempt
- The eavesdropper must then make a **Stealth check** to maintain concealment
- Successful eavesdropping requires an **unobstructed line of sight** to the conversation

If the Stealth check exceeds all Perception checks, the concealed character remains unseen and the conversation continues without knowledge of their presence.

---

## THEFT & TRESPASS

**Theft POIs** are required whenever an item — be it a decoration, flavor POI, piece of equipment, or otherwise — has been taken without permission, or an area or container has been entered by unauthorized means (picking a lock, breaking down a door, climbing a wall). This does **not** extend to mugging.

POIs must contain:
- The **method of entry** if applicable (lockpicking, breaching, climbing)
- The **item(s) taken**, if any

If the theft or trespass involved magic (Knock, Dimension Door, Mage Hand, etc.), the POI must also contain **magic POI requirements**, including the use of Knowledge: Arcana for tracking.

---

## POINT OF INTEREST

The thief may only steal from a residence when the rightful owner, or no fewer than **one occupant**, is online.

Thieves may cooperate with a maximum of **two other individuals** during joint operations. The collective must leave substantial POIs with all pertinent details.

### Required POI Information

- **Description of the Scene**
- **Discord Name**
- **Timestamp**
- **Date**
- **Tracking Modifier** (half of Survival, rounded up)

Individuals engaging in lockpicking or breaching must leave POIs at **every instance** of such attempts, regardless of outcome. All participants must leave at least one POI to demonstrate their involvement.

POIs must be left at:
- The **point of entrance**
- The **location from which theft occurs**
- The **point of exit** (if different from entrance)

Although only a single player's name is needed as a contact, **all clans represented** must be included on the POI.

For successful chest looting, POIs must include comprehensive information about each item stolen (quantity and type), documented via screenshots in your infiltration ticket.

> **Note:** POIs are for one-time use when utilized for tracking. After a single Tracking check — regardless of outcome — the POI is considered expended and should be removed.

---

## LOCKPICKING

If a character fails to pick a lock, they must execute a **Sleight of Hand check**. Any character within **8 tiles** of the lock may make a **Perception check** to detect the auditory disturbance.

- If the Perception check exceeds the Sleight of Hand check, the character may investigate the door
- The thief may then attempt to find concealment and make a new **Stealth check** to remain undetected
- A successful Stealth check becomes the **new Stealth DC** for the remainder of the infiltration

All lockpicking attempts during infiltrations/theft must be documented in your infiltration ticket.

---

## INFILTRATION

All base infiltration must align with the narrative of the story and facilitate immersive roleplay. An **infiltration ticket MUST be submitted BEFORE trespassing**. You do **NOT** need to wait for staff approval to proceed.

You must screenshot all of the following and include them in your ticket:
- Lockpicking attempts
- Breaching attempts
- Stealth rolls
- POIs placed

If theft is taking place, you **MUST** screenshot your inventory **BEFORE** entering the base upon ticket submission, and include the reason for your infiltration. Upon leaving, provide another screenshot including all items taken.

> Infiltration and theft tickets can and will be audited by staff. Failure to provide screenshots can result in return of stolen goods and possible disciplinary action.

For detailed infiltration rules (including hub infiltration, raids, and sieges), see the **Infiltration, Raid, & Siege Conflict Rules** page.

## THIEVING

Thieving is allowed on Aethrya.

**Before committing any theft**, the thief **MUST open an infiltration ticket**. If committed in a group, only one thief opens the ticket but must mention all thieves involved.

A clan/organization can only steal from another clan/organization **once per week**. However, they may steal from multiple different clans/organizations per week as long as each target has an elapsed time of one week since the last theft. Communicate with your clan members about which targets have been stolen from.

> An organization can include Factions, Gangs, Companies, Societies, Alliances, or any variation of multiple individuals with a common cause.

### Equipment Thievery

| Tier | Value |
|------|-------|
| Tier 1 Equipment | 2 |
| Tier 2 Equipment | 4 |
| Tier 3 Equipment | 6 |

### Resource Thievery

| Tier | Value |
|------|-------|
| Tier 1 Resources | 1 |
| Tier 2 Resources | 2 |
| Tier 3 Resources | 3 |

### Coin Thievery

| Tier | Maximum |
|------|---------|
| Tier 1 | Up to 10 silver |
| Tier 2 | Up to 20 silver |
| Tier 3 | Up to 30 silver (maximum allowed) |

Thieves are allowed to steal equipment and/or resources up to a **value of 6** from other clans and up to a **value of 9** from main faction hubs. Items stolen can be in any combination as long as the value limit is upheld.

---

## FLAVOR & INTEL THIEVERY

Flavor/intel items are physical objects offering no mechanical advantage — letters, POI-defined items, armor ToT on a stand, etc. — that would generate RP from their theft or removal.

These have their own separate limitations per infiltration:
- Up to **3 letters** and **3 other fluff items** per infiltration, in addition to the value limits for resource theft
- You may circumvent the letter limit by **transcribing** them — spend a minimum of **5 minutes per page or letter** copied
- Each transcription must include an emote posted in the infiltration ticket alongside a text submission of the information
- Private notices on boards, clan-use reports, etc. also require transcription

> **Sets:** Multiple items making up a set (chess set, silverware, bag of coins, ToT armor set) count as **one item** if the entire set is taken.

### Mechanical Items & Cooldowns

Mechanical items are defined as any economy item (raw materials, equipment runes, consumables). Stolen mechanical items must remain in the thief's inventory until tracking expires.

- During the **3-day tracking period**, stolen items may be retrieved regardless of combat ratio/consequence level
- Outside of that window, all normal PVP rules apply
- Players may hire mercenaries or seek third-party help for retrieval with appropriate RP
- **No stolen equipment may be equipped** until the 3-day window concludes

**Cooldowns:**
- Infiltrations with **no mechanical RPR item theft** (letters/fluff only): **3-day** cooldown
- Infiltrations with **mechanical RPR item theft** (crafting materials, armor, weapons, consumables): **7-day** cooldown

## MUGGING

When a thief or group of thieves attempts to mug a character:

- A single character may take equipment and/or resources up to a **value of 3**, **OR** up to a maximum of **30 silver**
- The mugged character **cannot receive any additional consequences** for this scene

### Mugging in a Group

Each thief may only take from a **single individual** up to a value of 3 and/or up to 30 silver. Thieves **cannot take from the same victim**.

> **Example:** If three thieves attack a group of two characters, only two thieves can receive stolen items.

If a character is **kidnapped or captured** as a PvP consequence, they must unequip their runes. Equipment and carried items are **not usable** during imprisonment — only after being returned, acquired another way, or the character is freed.

---

## THEFT AS CONSEQUENCE

After a PvP encounter, the victor may claim **theft as consequence** and loot the downed character:

- Equipment and/or resources up to a **value of 3**, and/or up to a maximum of **30 silver**
- The downed character **cannot receive any additional consequences** for this scene

If the victor(s) decide to **kidnap/capture** the downed character:
- The victim must unequip runes; equipment is not usable during imprisonment
- **Do not actually remove items from the player's inventory** — they simply may not equip or use them

### Use of Body Parts Taken as Consequences

1. Body parts or bodies must be indicated with a **POI** when moved to a new location, identifying the body and any rituals (Gentle Repose, etc.) cast on them
2. If a player rerolls while in possession of a body part, they may:
   - Pass the body part to another active character, **OR**
   - Bring it with them (in this case, it is considered **destroyed** for resurrection purposes)
3. Corpses can be located via **Locate Object** but **cannot be scried**
4. If a corpse is cut into pieces, you may specify which piece you are casting Locate Object on (e.g., "Player Name's Head"), or generalize — it will target the **nearest legal fragment**
5. Bodies/body parts not preserved by Gentle Repose are considered **destroyed after 30 IRL days**
6. Bodies and body parts may **not** be stored in demiplanes
7. Player bodies may **not** be reanimated into undead

## TRACKING THIEVERY POIs

Whenever any thievery system is utilized, **POIs and Tracking information** must be provided by the thief. This ensures the seamless continuation of roleplay.

- POIs must adhere to all standard conditions outlined in the **Tracking & POIs** rules
- On a successful Tracking attempt, the thief must share their movements up to the **border of the current zone**
- If the thief crosses a zone border after leaving a POI, an **additional tracking roll** is required at that border
- Upon a successful result, the thief must provide movements after crossing the border

> **VPN Rule:** Once the thief crosses the **first zone border**, no further tracking assessments are required if the thief crosses subsequent zone borders.

> **Important:** Limit tracking attempts to **one per Point of Interest**.
