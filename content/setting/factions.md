# FACTIONS OF AETHRYA

## Three Kingdoms, Three Visions, One Fractured World

---

In Aethrya, the traditional power structures of the Forgotten Realms have collapsed and reformed into something new. The old alliances, the Lords' Alliance, the Harpers, the Zhentarim — they exist as echoes, fragmented memories of a copied world.

What has risen in their place are the **Three Kingdoms** — sovereign city-states that anchor civilization along the Sword Coast North. Each represents a fundamentally different answer to the same question:

> *How do you survive in a world that was never meant to exist-*

---

## LEILON — THE IRON BASTION

**Banner:** A clenched gauntlet over a cracked shield
**Seat of Power:** The Steel Citadel
**Government:** The Military Council

### Identity

Leilon answers with **order**. In a world where reality itself fractures, the Iron Bastion believes that only the unyielding discipline of mortal will can hold the line. Laws are strict, roles are defined, and the chain of command is absolute.

### Leadership

The Military Council is a triumvirate of generals — one for War, one for Infrastructure, and one for Intelligence. Their identities are public, but their inner deliberations are not. Decisions are swift and final. Dissent is tolerated in private. In public, unity is everything.

Rumors persist that one member of the Council is not what they appear to be — that something ancient wears a general's face. These rumors are never spoken aloud twice.

### Military Doctrine

Leilon fields the most organized fighting force on the Sword Coast:

- **The Wardguard** — professional soldiers who patrol the city and its surrounding territory
- **The Steel Vanguard** — elite forces deployed for external threats, raids, and expeditions
- **The Silent Watch** — intelligence operatives whose existence is officially denied

### Relations

| Faction | Stance |
|---------|--------|
| **Phandalin** | Tense but functional — Leilon views Phandalin's openness as a security risk, but values its trade |
| **Sombersky** | Deep mistrust — Leilon's generals view the Veilkeepers' secrets as a potential threat to stability |

### Themes

- Authoritarian order vs. personal freedom
- The cost of security
- The danger of blind obedience
- What hides behind the mask of control

---

## PHANDALIN — THE FREE HAVEN

**Banner:** An open hand beneath a rising star
**Seat of Power:** The Commons Hall
**Government:** The Commons Assembly

### Identity

Phandalin answers with **freedom**. Where Leilon builds walls, Phandalin opens doors. It is a haven for the displaced, the curious, the ambitious, and the desperate — a melting pot of cultures, races, and ideas where the only cardinal sin is refusing to live and let live.

### Leadership

The Commons Assembly is an elected body of representatives drawn from Phandalin's guilds, trade houses, and community wards. Elections occur every six months, and any citizen of Phandalin may stand. The system is messy, loud, often corrupt — and fiercely defended by its people.

No single figure dominates the Assembly for long. Influence rises and falls with the markets, the seasons, and the crises. This makes Phandalin adaptable but sometimes paralyzed at the worst possible moment.

### Civic Institutions

- **The Merchant Guilds** — powerful trade organizations that control the flow of goods along the Sword Coast
- **The Inquirers' Circle** — scholars, sages, and researchers dedicated to understanding the Fracturing
- **The Freeblades** — a registered guild of mercenaries and adventurers who take on contracts for the city

### Relations

| Faction | Stance |
|---------|--------|
| **Leilon** | Cautious cooperation — Phandalin needs Leilon's military strength but resents its authoritarianism |
| **Sombersky** | Fascination and frustration — Phandalin's scholars want access to the Veilkeepers' knowledge; the Veilkeepers rarely share |

### Themes

- Freedom vs. responsibility
- The dangers of unchecked openness
- Knowledge as currency and weapon
- What grows when you let everything in

---

## SOMBERSKY — THE VEILED THRONE

**Banner:** A crescent moon behind storm clouds
**Seat of Power:** The Cathedral of Dimming Light
**Government:** The Veilkeepers

### Identity

Sombersky answers with **faith**. Not faith in a single god — the gods are bound, after all — but faith in the *idea* that meaning persists even in a world that shouldn't exist. The Veiled Throne is a city of ritual, contemplation, and quiet power. Its people believe they are custodians of something sacred.

They may be right. The Veil is thinner in Sombersky than anywhere else on the Sword Coast. Prayers are answered more often — though the answers are not always kind.

### Leadership

The Veilkeepers are a theocratic council of priests, seers, and mystics drawn from multiple faith traditions. They do not worship a single deity but instead tend to what they call **"The Residue"** — the lingering divine energy that bleeds through the Veil.

Membership in the Veilkeepers is not elected. It is *revealed*. Candidates are identified through prophecy, vision, and ritual. This makes the council deeply influential — and impossible to infiltrate through conventional means.

### Sacred Orders

- **The Twilight Wardens** — guardians of the Cathedral and the Veilglass Spires, trained in both martial and spiritual disciplines
- **The Listeners** — mystics who monitor the Veil for signs of divine activity or reality-fracture
- **The Ashen Choir** — ritualists who perform the ceremonies believed to slow the Fracturing in Sombersky's territory

### Relations

| Faction | Stance |
|---------|--------|
| **Leilon** | Cold distance — Sombersky views Leilon's military posture as crude and spiritually hollow |
| **Phandalin** | Guarded respect — the Veilkeepers appreciate Phandalin's curiosity but fear what its scholars might do with the wrong knowledge |

### Themes

- Faith without certainty
- The burden of sacred knowledge
- Theocracy vs. individual belief
- What it means to protect a world you know is a copy

## INTER-FACTION DYNAMICS

The three kingdoms exist in a state of permanent, uneasy equilibrium:

- **Leilon** and **Phandalin** compete for economic and military dominance along the trade routes
- **Phandalin** and **Sombersky** vie for intellectual authority over the nature of the Fracturing
- **Sombersky** and **Leilon** clash over the role of spirituality in governance

No kingdom has the strength to dominate the others. None can afford to. The Sword Coast's survival depends on their fractious cooperation — even as each secretly prepares for the day that cooperation fails.

### The Neutral Territories

The Wilds between the kingdoms are contested ground — claimed by none, settled by many. Adventurers, outcasts, and independent clans make their homes in these untamed regions, owing allegiance to no faction.

This is where most player stories begin.

---

## CHOOSING YOUR PATH

When you arrive in Aethrya, you are free to:

- **Align with a faction** — joining the ranks of Leilon, Phandalin, or Sombersky and rising through their structures
- **Remain independent** — settling in the Wilds, forming your own clan, charting your own course
- **Play the factions against each other** — navigating the political landscape for profit, power, or survival

There is no wrong answer — only consequences.

> *"Every traveler who reaches the Sword Coast faces the same choice: serve the kingdoms, stand apart from them, or try to change them from within. The only certainty is that none of them will be the same by the time you leave."*
> — Phandalin street proverb
