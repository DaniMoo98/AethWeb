# LORETONS

## Echoes of a World That Shouldn't Remember

---

## WHAT IS A LORETON-

A **Loreton** is a physical manifestation of Aethrya's fractured memory — a tangible piece of the world's broken history that has crystallized into existence.

When the Primordials copied the Forgotten Realms to create Aethrya, the duplication was imperfect. Entire chapters of history were compressed, scrambled, or split. Events that should exist as linear memory instead collapsed into physical objects, locations, or phenomena.

These are Loretons.

> *"Imagine a moment in time so significant that the world couldn't simply copy it. Instead, it fell out of the timeline and became... a thing. A place. An event that keeps happening."*
> — From the Archive of Echoes, Researcher's Note 47-C

---

## TYPES OF LORETONS

### Object Loretons

Artifacts that contain compressed historical memory. When touched, studied, or attuned to, they can reveal fragments of Aethrya's hidden past — or the original Forgotten Realms' history before the copy was made.

- A sword that remembers a battle no one else does
- A book written in a language that hasn't been invented yet
- A coin minted by a ruler who never existed in this timeline

**Properties:**
- Objects may resist identification through normal magical means
- Extended contact can cause **memory bleed** — the holder begins experiencing events from the object's compressed history
- Some Object Loretons are inert until a specific condition is met

### Location Loretons

Places where history has *pooled*. These locations don't merely contain relics of the past — they *are* the past, partially overlapping with the present.

- A clearing in the forest where it is always raining, because in another timeline, a great storm struck here and never stopped
- A ruin that appears fully intact under moonlight, revealing the structure as it existed before its fall
- A stretch of road where travelers hear the sounds of an army marching — an army from a war that occurred only in the original Realms

**Properties:**
- Location Loretons are semi-permanent but may shift or fade
- They are often surrounded by weak reality — minor Fracturing events are common nearby
- The Veilkeepers of Sombersky actively catalog and monitor known Location Loretons

### Event Loretons

The rarest and most dangerous type. An Event Loreton is a moment in time that has become *stuck* — endlessly repeating, partially manifesting, or bleeding into the present without warning.

- A marketplace where, every seventh day, the crowd is briefly replaced by people from another era
- A hilltop where a ritual of unknown origin plays out in shadow every full moon
- A battlefield where the dead rise not as undead, but as *memories* — phantom soldiers fighting a war that may not have happened

**Properties:**
- Event Loretons are unpredictable and often dangerous
- They cannot be dispelled through conventional magic
- Proximity to an Event Loreton may cause temporary memory alteration or time displacement

## LORETONS & THE FRACTURING

Loretons are both symptoms and amplifiers of the Fracturing.

Each Loreton represents a point where the Fracturing has already occurred — where the boundary between Aethrya's copied existence and the original timeline has weakened enough for history itself to leak through.

### The Cycle

1. A Fracturing event occurs, weakening reality
2. Compressed history crystallizes into a Loreton
3. The Loreton further destabilizes the surrounding area
4. More Fracturing events occur nearby
5. The cycle accelerates

The First Resonance dramatically increased the rate of Loreton formation across the Sword Coast. Loretons that had been dormant for years reactivated. New ones appeared overnight.

## INTERACTING WITH LORETONS

### Discovery

Loretons can be found through:

- **Exploration** — encountering them in the Wilds, the Underdark, or within settlements
- **Research** — scholars at the Archive of Echoes and the Veilkeepers maintain partial catalogs
- **DM Events** — major story beats may involve the discovery or activation of significant Loretons
- **Divine Interference** — Fragment-bearers and those sensitive to the Veil may be drawn to Loretons instinctively

### Study & Use

- Object Loretons can be carried, studied, and potentially attuned to
- Location Loretons can be explored, mapped, and exploited for resources or knowledge
- Event Loretons should be approached with extreme caution — observation is recommended; interaction is at your own risk

### Faction Perspectives

| Faction | Approach to Loretons |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Leilon** | Views them as tactical assets or security threats. Secures and quarantines when possible. |
| **Phandalin** | Views them as research subjects and sources of knowledge. Advocates for public study. |
| **Sombersky** | Views them as sacred fragments of divine memory. Protects and performs rituals around them. |

## A NOTE TO ADVENTURERS

Loretons are not treasure to be hoarded.

They are the scars of a world trying to remember what it was before it was copied. Some hold answers. Some hold warnings. Some hold nothing but grief.

Approach them with curiosity, caution, and respect.

And if a Loreton shows you a memory that feels like *your own* —

Walk away.

> *"The world remembers. The question is whether those memories are Aethrya's... or something else's entirely."*
> — Sombersky Listener, name withheld
