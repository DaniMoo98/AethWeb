# THE SWORD COAST OF AETHRYA

## A Familiar Shore, Refracted Through Broken Glass

---

The Sword Coast of Aethrya stretches along the western edge of Faerûn — or what *should* be Faerûn. It mirrors its original form in geography, topology, and coastline, but the details are wrong in ways that unsettle those who look too closely.

Roads lead to places that didn't exist yesterday. Forests grow in directions they shouldn't. The stars are *almost* right.

This is the nature of a reflection: it shows you what you expect to see — until you look at it from the wrong angle.

---

## THE THREE KINGDOMS

Three great cities anchor civilization along the Sword Coast North. Each began as settlements familiar to any traveler of the Realms, but within Aethrya, they have grown into sovereign powers — fractured reflections of what they once were, reshaped by the instability of this copied world.

### Leilon — The Iron Bastion

*"Where order is carved from chaos, stone by stone."*

Leilon stands where it always has — at the crossroads of the High Road and the coast — but in Aethrya, it has grown into something greater. What was once a modest town is now a fortified city-state, its walls reinforced by generations of siege and storm.

- **Government:** Military Council — ruled by a triumvirate of generals and marshals
- **Philosophy:** Strength through discipline. Law through force. Survival through unity.
- **Character:** Leilon values order above all else. Its streets are patrolled, its citizens documented, its defenses legendary. But beneath the iron discipline lies a growing fracture — whispers that the Council is controlled by something that is not mortal.
- **Key Features:**
  - The **Steel Citadel** — seat of the Military Council, said to have existed before the city was built
  - The **Wardwall** — a shimmering barrier around the inner city that predates any known enchantment
  - The **Forge Quarter** — where smiths craft weapons from metals that don't exist in nature
- **Notable Tensions:** Leilon's rigid structure makes it a natural enemy of chaos — and a natural prison for those who refuse to conform. Desertion is punished harshly. Questions about the Council's true nature are punished more harshly still.

---

### Phandalin — The Free Haven

*"Where every voice carries weight, and every secret has a price."*

Phandalin was always a town of second chances. In Aethrya, that identity has crystallized into something deliberate and dangerous. The town has swelled into a sprawling trade city — a haven for adventurers, outcasts, refugees, and those who prefer questions to answers.

- **Government:** The Commons Assembly — an elected council of representatives, merchants, and guild leaders
- **Philosophy:** Freedom through commerce. Truth through inquiry. Power through knowledge.
- **Character:** Phandalin thrives on openness and trade. It welcomes all races, all creeds, all walks of life. But this openness is also its greatest vulnerability — spies move freely, cults take root easily, and the line between freedom and lawlessness blurs daily.
- **Key Features:**
  - The **Crossroads Market** — the largest open trading hub on the Sword Coast, where anything can be bought or sold
  - The **Archive of Echoes** — a library rumored to contain books from both Aethrya *and* the original timeline
  - The **Wanderer's Gate** — the only standing portal confirmed to flicker between realities
- **Notable Tensions:** Phandalin's tolerance makes it a breeding ground for Fragment-seekers and reality cults. The Assembly struggles to balance freedom with the growing threat of those who would exploit the Fracturing.

---

### Sombersky — The Veiled Throne

*"Where faith endures in shadow, and the old ways remember."*

Sombersky does not appear on maps of the original Forgotten Realms. It *should not exist*. And yet it stands — a city of pale stone and perpetual twilight, nestled in the foothills where the mountains meet the sea.

- **Government:** The Veilkeepers — a theocratic council of priests, seers, and mystics
- **Philosophy:** Preservation through faith. Wisdom through silence. Protection through mystery.
- **Character:** Sombersky is a city of whispered prayers and ancient rituals. Its people believe they are closer to the gods than any other settlement — and they may be right. The Veil is thinner here. Miracles are more common. But so are the consequences.
- **Key Features:**
  - The **Cathedral of Dimming Light** — a temple to no single god, where the boundaries between mortal and divine blur
  - The **Pale Markets** — where trade occurs in candlelight, and the currency includes secrets and favors
  - The **Veilglass Spires** — towers of crystallized reality-fracture, used by the Veilkeepers for divination
- **Notable Tensions:** Sombersky's mysticism makes it both revered and feared. The Veilkeepers guard knowledge of the Veil and the Fracturing fiercely, which puts them at odds with Phandalin's scholars and Leilon's generals alike.

## KEY LOCATIONS

Beyond the three kingdoms, the Sword Coast of Aethrya is dotted with locations both familiar and impossible.

### The Underdark

The tunnels beneath Aethrya mirror those of the original Realms, but with significant aberrations. Entire sections of the Underdark phase in and out of existence. Tunnels that existed yesterday may not tomorrow. The deeper you go, the more unstable reality becomes.

- The Underdark is accessible through multiple cave systems along the Sword Coast
- Certain sections are claimed by drow, duergar, and other subterranean peoples
- Deep exploration risks encounters with **reality tears** — points where Aethrya's copy begins to fail

### The Wilds

The forests, hills, and coastal lowlands between the three kingdoms are collectively known as the Wilds. These untamed regions are home to:

- Wandering monsters and displaced creatures
- Ruins that predate their own construction
- Gathering zones rich in crafting materials
- Temporary camps and outposts established by adventurers

### Gathering Zones & Warps

Scattered across the Sword Coast are locations of practical importance:

- **Gathering Zones** — resource-rich areas where professions harvest materials
- **Warps** — unstable spatial folds that allow rapid travel between regions (a side effect of the Fracturing that mortals have learned to exploit)

These are considered protected zones — see the **Combat Rules** for details on engagement restrictions near these locations.

## THE NATURE OF THE COAST

The Sword Coast of Aethrya is a place of contrasts:

- Civilization struggling against cosmic instability
- Three kingdoms with fundamentally different visions for survival
- An Underdark that defies mapping
- Wilds that grow stranger by the season

It is a land where heroes are forged — not because the world needs saving, but because it was never meant to exist at all.

And yet, mortals *insist* on living here.

> *"The Sword Coast is not a frontier. It is a scar. And we are what grows in the wound."*
> — Attributed to a Sombersky Veilkeeper, identity unknown
