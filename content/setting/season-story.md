# SEASON STORY

## The Awakening — Season One

---

## PROLOGUE: THE CALM BEFORE THE CRACK

For as long as anyone remembers, the Sword Coast has been a place of struggle — but *manageable* struggle. Bandits on the roads. Monsters in the hills. Political tension between the three kingdoms.

Normal things. Expected things.

Until recently.

---

## THE INCITING EVENT

Three months ago, a tremor shook the Sword Coast — not of the earth, but of *reality itself*.

For a single, terrifying moment:
- The sky split into two overlapping skies
- Every person on the Coast experienced a memory that was not their own
- Time in Sombersky ran backward for six seconds
- A tower in Leilon briefly existed in two places at once

The Veilkeepers called it **The First Resonance** — the largest single Fracturing event in recorded history.

Then it stopped.

The world snapped back to normal, or what passes for normal in Aethrya. Officials in all three kingdoms dismissed it as a magical disturbance. Scholars in Phandalin published theories. The Veilkeepers of Sombersky said nothing at all.

But those who were paying attention noticed:

> Something changed after the Resonance. Something in the *air*. In the ground. In the way the gods feel when you pray.

---

## THE CURRENT STATE OF THE SWORD COAST

### Leilon — Tightening the Grip

In the wake of the First Resonance, the Military Council has declared a state of heightened readiness. Patrols are doubled. Border checks are stricter. Outsiders are watched more carefully.

Officially, this is precautionary.

Unofficially, the Council is searching for something. Agents of the Silent Watch have been spotted in the Wilds, far beyond Leilon's usual sphere of influence. They are looking for **Divine Fragments** — and they are not being subtle about it.

### Phandalin — The Rush for Answers

Phandalin's Inquirers' Circle has mobilized in force. Researchers flood the Archive of Echoes, seeking references to the Resonance in texts both old and impossibly new. The Wanderer's Gate — the portal known to flicker between realities — has been placed under round-the-clock observation.

Meanwhile, the aethrya Market buzzes with a new kind of trade: **Fragment rumors**. Traders sell maps to suspected Fragment sites. Mercenaries offer their services as Fragment escorts. Cults promising Fragment-based salvation have tripled in the last month.

The Assembly debates regulation. The debates go nowhere.

### Sombersky — The Silence

Sombersky has gone quiet.

The Cathedral of Dimming Light closed its doors to outsiders the day after the Resonance. The Listeners have retreated into deep meditation. The Ashen Choir has begun performing rituals not seen in decades.

The city's message to the outside world is simple:

> *"We are praying. Do not disturb us."*

What they are praying *for* — or *to* — is unknown.

## THE SEASON ARC: THREADS OF FATE

Season One explores the aftermath of the First Resonance through multiple interweaving story threads:

### Thread 1: The Fragment Hunt

Divine Fragments have begun appearing in greater numbers since the Resonance. They surface in ruins, in the Underdark, in the Wilds — and sometimes in people.

- Factions race to secure Fragments for their own purposes
- Independent adventurers compete for Fragment bounties
- Fragment-bearers begin to emerge — mortals bonded to divine power, changing in ways they cannot control

### Thread 2: The Veil Weakens

The Veil — the metaphysical law binding the gods into mortal form — is showing signs of strain. Miracles are becoming less subtle. Divine power bleeds more freely.

Somewhere on the Sword Coast, a god is beginning to remember.

- Strange individuals appear who seem to know things they shouldn't
- Divine magic behaves erratically — prayers are answered by the wrong deity, or answered *too well*
- The Veilkeepers grow increasingly alarmed

### Thread 3: Political Fractures

The three kingdoms, always in tension, are being pushed toward confrontation by the Resonance:

- Leilon demands a military coalition to secure the Fragments — under its command
- Phandalin insists on academic study and public access
- Sombersky refuses to share its knowledge of the Veil

Trust erodes. Alliances shift. Independents in the Wilds are caught in the middle.

### Thread 4: The Primordial Question

Beneath all the politics and chaos, a deeper question stirs:

> *Why now-*

The Primordials have been absent since the creation of Aethrya. But the Resonance felt *intentional*. As if something reached in and *shook* the world to see what would fall out.

Scattered reports emerge of symbols matching no known language, carved into locations where Fragments appeared. The symbols are identical — but no two people describe the same number of them.

## WHERE YOUR STORY BEGINS

You arrive on the Sword Coast in the wake of the First Resonance.

The world is buzzing with uncertainty, opportunity, and fear. The kingdoms are mobilizing. The Fragments are rising. The gods are stirring.

You carry no prophecy. You are not chosen.

You are simply *here* — in a world that wasn't supposed to exist, at a moment when everything might change.

What you do next is up to you.
