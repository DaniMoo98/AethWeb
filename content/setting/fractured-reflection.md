# AETHRYA — THE FRACTURED REFLECTION OF EXISTENCE

## The Sword Coast of a Broken Reality

---

## I. The Fracture of Time

Aethrya is not a world born.

It is a world *copied*.

A perfect, terrible echo of existence — torn from the fabric of reality itself.

In an age before recorded time, before even the oldest gods recall, three Prime Primordials — entities older than creation, older than the Weave, older than thought itself — awoke beyond the boundaries of existence.

They are remembered only in fragments, forbidden texts, and broken prophecy:

- **The First Silence** — The One Who Ended What Was
- **The Shaper of Reflections** — The One Who Made What Should Not Be
- **The Boundless Hunger** — The One Who Devours Continuity

Together, they enacted an impossible act:

> They split time.

Not forward. Not backward.

**Sideways.**

From the living timeline of the Forgotten Realms, they carved a duplicate strand — an entire mirrored existence — and named it nothing.

Mortals would later call it **Aethrya**.

---

## II. The Binding of the Pantheon

The Primordials did not merely copy the world.

They **bound its gods**.

The Faerûnian pantheon — beings of immense power, worship, and dominion — were not destroyed, nor removed...

They were **sealed into limitation**.

A universal law was imposed upon Aethrya:

> **No god may walk as a god.**

Each deity was forced into a **Polymorphic Veil**, a shifting mortal form that suppresses their divine essence.

- Their memories fracture over time
- Their powers bleed slowly into the world
- Their identities become unstable

They walk among mortals now:

- As kings and beggars
- As merchants and mercenaries
- As lovers, tyrants, prophets, and monsters

Some remember what they are.

Most do not.

---

## III. The Veil of the Living Gods

This condition is known as **The Veil** — a metaphysical law that governs all divine existence within Aethrya.

### Effects of the Veil

- Divine power manifests in subtle, unstable ways
- Miracles are rare, often mistaken for coincidence
- Direct divine intervention is impossible without consequence
- True forms can only emerge during catastrophic reality strain

Should a god attempt to fully reclaim their divinity:

> The world itself resists.

Reality fractures further. Time stutters. Entire regions may collapse into paradox.

---

## IV. The Sword Coast of Aethrya

The Sword Coast exists as a familiar yet deeply altered reflection of its original form.

Cities stand where they should.

Histories resemble truth.

But nothing is *correct*.

### Key Differences

- Political powers shift unpredictably
- Forgotten events reappear as "new" history
- Entire bloodlines exist that should not
- Ruins predate their own construction

This is the nature of a copied world:

> It is not built to be stable.

---

## V. The Divine Fragments

With the gods bound and their power suppressed, something began to leak.

Cracks in reality formed.

From these cracks emerged **Divine Fragments** — residual shards of godhood, raw and unfiltered divine essence.

These fragments can:

- Bond to mortals
- Grant immense, unstable power
- Alter fate, form, and identity

However, they come at a cost:

- Madness
- Identity erosion
- Attraction of other fragments — and worse

Only a rare few can withstand them. Fewer still can control them.

---

## VI. The Primordials' Purpose

Why did the Primordials create Aethrya-

No unified answer exists.

Theories include:

- **Experimentation** — Testing the limits of divinity without gods
- **Harvest** — Cultivating divine energy through mortal struggle
- **Containment** — Imprisoning the pantheon to prevent a greater threat
- **Consumption** — Slowly devouring reality through instability

What *is* known:

> Aethrya is not meant to last forever.

---

## VII. The Instability of Reality

Aethrya suffers from increasing fractures:

- Time loops and skips
- Memory inconsistencies across populations
- Locations phasing in and out of existence
- Individuals encountering alternate versions of themselves

These phenomena are collectively known as **The Fracturing**.

Each event weakens the boundary between Aethrya and the original timeline.

If the barrier collapses:

- Both realities may merge
- One may overwrite the other
- Or both may cease entirely

---

## VIII. The Mortal Era

Despite its origin, Aethrya now belongs to mortals.

The gods walk unseen. The Primordials remain absent.

Power lies not in divinity — but in those who dare to claim it.

### Current Age Themes

- Hidden divinity among the populace
- Political struggle masking cosmic truth
- Rise of Fragment-bearers
- Secret wars between disguised gods
- Discovery of forbidden knowledge

---

## IX. The Truth Beneath All Things

There is one truth few understand:

> Aethrya is not a world.

It is a **mistake that continues to exist**.

And something — whether Primordial, divine, or mortal —

Is beginning to notice.
