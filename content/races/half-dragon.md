## Race Information

### Society

#### Of Scale, Tail, and Traveled Tales

Inheriting varying levels of influence from their draconic parenthood, all half-dragons present formidable features of their great reptilian ancestry. This includes horns, scales, claws, a tail, eyes which pierce the night, and even the fabled projection of a dragon's breath, all dependent on the nature of their draconic lineage. So strong is this natural connection to their mighty skybound ancestry that it may manifest in awakened sorcery within a half-dragon, granting them power beyond the bounds of their physicality and granting them a grasp over the Weave all their own.

Often the result of a dragon's pursuit of either gaining a capable servant to its territorial grasp or standing as a lasting legacy of its life lived, half-dragons may find themselves raised either in the throes of their draconic parentage or among their mortal peers. Being birthed between two worlds leaves its mark on those born of half-dragon descent, though it need not dictate their characteristics. They may take after either side of their ancestry of their own volition, as well, should they ever choose to leave what they came to know. In either case, the avaricious nature of dragons often is carried in those birthed by them, as well as traits owed to the variety of draconic heritage. Gold half-dragons may wish to protect the weak, or take advantage of such assumptions made by others for personal gain. Red half-dragons might wish to assert themselves over any who may offer them personal gain, or defy such callings in the name of defending fellow outcasts.

Half-dragons are often the first face to garner attention entering any room, given their unnatural and striking appearances. Even those of a more subtle draconic visage would easily be spotted in any crowd, and rarely go without some manner of ridicule or greater notice as a result. Many among the realms fear them for their capacity of destruction - a lasting impression of their longer-lived parents that all would do well to remember, namely the chromatic variety - but those of metallic sorts may find themselves gaining the eyes of the needy and weary as well. As well, there exist both the Cult of the Dragon and the Cult of Tiamat, each who would gladly take malicious or unawares half-dragons beneath their wings in the name of villainous acts in the name of malicious dragonkind.

Found within any locale where men and dragons may meet, many half-dragons reside in settlements and regions in which the two reside mutually. Such places include the Kryptgarden Forest of the Lost Hills, Threskel of Chessentea, the High Country of Rashemen, and Battledale of the Dalelands as examples, though any greater region where dragons may dwell, so too do their often estranged offspring. ### Regions

##### The Great and the Lost

Given the unique nature of their origins in addition to the territorial nature of true dragons, half-dragons claim no specific corner of the realms as their true home. Each lives a life all their own and with a story fittingly intriguing to their roots, though there do remain some locales all the same in which pockets of these wayward beings find themselves. Additionally, the numerous cities of Faerûn and beyond may find themselves with some slim number of their kind, scarcely missed even in the midst of bustling streets - from backgrounds as humble as a traveling merchant with a silver tongue to inspiring members of militia groups, half-dragons may fit any lifestyle, though tend to find themselves in positions of adventure and influence of some sort by nature.

##### Half-Dragons of the Sword Coast

Taking after the many various features of their draconic parent, any half-dragon could find a habitat suited to its capabilities and interests, though the greater the number of dragons, the greater the odds of half-dragon presence would be. Fittingly, the Kryptgarden Forest of the Lost Hills proves to be a suitable region for half-dragons of many varying backgrounds. Between the efforts of the Cult of the Dragon within the region in the year 1489 DR, havens such as Leilon or Phandalin which readily welcome adventurers, or simply a result of one among the primarily green dragons in the area, half-dragons may come from any background or coastal settlement neighboring these storied and vicious woodlands.

##### Half-Dragons of the Dalelands

Nestled between the untamed Desertsmouth Mountains and the Dragon Reach fjord, the Dales alongside Cormanthor house a veritable trove of half-dragons throughout the region - as large of a trove that such a rarity of existence could, at least. The fabled ruins of Myth Drannor, long guarded and fought for by dragonkind of all sorts between its many invasions and conflicts, stand as a prominent point of magnetism for the winged lords of the sky, additionally proving a suitable proving grounds for the most daring of half-dragons to venture forth and seek great fortune. The peoples of the Dalelands are hardy, tested and resilient in the wake of life so rife with threats to their daily lives - nonetheless, many thrived in the farmlands sown and tended to amidst the dangers lurking in the forests surrounding themselves. In the towering heights of the Desertsmouth Mountains, the lich Sammaster - responsible both for the founding of the Cult of the Dragon as well as discovering the process of raising a dragon into a dracolich - was active and gathering forces within the region as of 1282 DR, though he and many of his forces were slain once more in 1285 DR.

### Appearance

**Draconic:** No matter the mortal half of their origins, half-dragons take after their draconic parentage in ways which influence both their characteristics and their physical forms. Specific to appearance, varying levels of draconic scales cover their bodies in addition to eyes reminiscent of their monstrous heritage. Horns sprout and shape themselves atop the crowns of their heads, and it's far more common than not that a tail accompanies those of a true dragon's bloodline. Claws on both the hands and feet are often fully visible and sharp enough to serve as cutting tools or impromptu weapons as well. Each and every one of these features retains coloration borne of their draconic parentage, though the severity of them changes from one half-dragon to the next. Some may carry faces not so distant from their mortal ancestry, whilst others would be mistaken for a tailed and towering dragonborn at a glance.

**Outstanding:** Any and all half-dragons are easily and capably discerned from commonfolk by their more reptilian features, though it would be little surprise that they stand apart even from the likes of dragonborn or draconic sorcerers in an instant as well. The blood of true dragons shows itself in sharp, piercing eyes, greater height garnered by the larger of their two parents, horns larger and more grandiose than many among other horned folk in the realms, as well as frills and sharpened teeth. None but the blind could miss such features, and even still, an ill-fated meeting of bodies in a runaway bump would inform a blind man of much, given the sharpness and durability of even a half-dragon's scales.

**Mighty:** Among the many boons half-dragons carry due to their lineage are larger and bulkier forms. Granted a portion of a dragon's incredible strength by birthright, those of such powerful descent enjoy an innate brawn and resilience which few other creatures of humanoid ancestry can boast.

#### Varied Appearances

##### Red Half-Dragons

Are known to be red or crimson in the hue of their scales, bearing lengthy and sometimes slightly curved horns of many colors, though namely a strong and solid black or white.

##### Gold Half-Dragons

Are known to carry bright and reflective golden scales, with horns atop of their heads of metallic appearance.

##### Green Half-Dragons

Typically possess scales of verdant coloration, as well as shorter horns atop several joint locations of their outer facial structure.

### Faith

#### Bahamut, The Platinum Dragon

The Grandfather of Dragons stands as a devout and steadfast guardian of justice, serving Torm directly as perhaps the very definition of compassion at his side. Lawful good in his nature, Bahamut carries many monickers and titles - Angel of the Seven Heavens, Grand Master of Flowers, Lord of the North Wind, and the Justicemaker to name a handful. Though he leads good-aligned metallic dragons of his own creation by a caring and considerate example for the woes of mortals, Bahamut spares no patience for the vile or the malignant. Ever ready to meet the tide of darkness wherever it rises, he acts to prevent foes from performing acts of malice again, preferably without bloodshed. Half-dragons may look to Bahamut for guidance in life, as well as to serve valiantly as warriors of honor and the defense of the meek.

#### Tiamat, The Dragon Queen

Sister and opposing mirror to Bahamut in nearly every way, Tiamat is the five-headed progenitor of chromatic dragons the realms over. Spiteful, avaricious to a fault, and ever seeking greater power, the Queen of Chaos boasts formidable might and relations among the archfiends of Baator, the Nine Hells, in addition to the Church of Tiamat and Cult of the Dragon performing sacrifice and bloodshed in her name across Faerûn. The Dark Lady plots and fiercely wars for a greater hold in any corner of the Material Prime where she may find it, even recently nearly being released from the clutches of Avernus by the Cult. Ultimately being met with failure, however, Tiamat and her many followers continue to ransack and destroy any who would oppose her prophesied victory, all the while gathering what might she may and all of the wealth a dragon could possibly hope to hoard.

#### Waukeen, The Coinmaiden

While not a deity dragons would concern themselves with at any frequent rate, the Merchantsfriend stands as a symbol of wealth and the value of improving society through investment among the common men and women of Faerûn. Venerated primarily by merchants, entrepreneurs of all walks of life were as welcomed in her gaze, even lowly peddlers. The art of the trade and collection of coin through decent exchange fall under Our Lady of Gold's domain, and as such, any half-dragon with a penchant to build their own trove of treasure - or at least maintain filled coffers - may turn to Waukeen as an ideal symbol of their belief in seeking to reach the top of the heap through trade.
