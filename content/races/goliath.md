## Race Information

### Society

#### The People Of The Peaks

Every goliath has three names. Their first name is given to them by their parents, their last name their family or clan name. This last name always ends in a vowel. Their middle name is a nickname assigned to them by the tribal chief that typically describes some trait of theirs or a significant event. Walking amongst a goliath tribe, one would hear them refer to each other as 'Elkfriend', 'Cloudseeker', 'Yetipuncher' in casual conversation. In introductions and more serious scenarios, they will use their full name.

Goliath tribes are shamanistic, revering nature with a deep respect for its primal power. Rare is the goliath tribe that does not have a druid among them, who invokes the spirits of nature and their ancestors for counsel. They steward the connection between their tribe members and these spirits. These druids guide with their wisdom, forecast the weather, and ensure the tribe does not take more from the land than they need.

Their tribes are typically no more than 100 people. Tribes that wander more frequently tend to be smaller. Every tribe has its chieftain, who usually got their position by defeating the previous chieftain in three contests. A grave responsibility, goliaths do not challenge their chieftains lightly. The tribe's skywatcher is a chief druid who monitors hunting and gathering, and prepares festivals and rites. Adjudicators act as referees for games and mediators for disputes. Dawncallers are bards of the tribe who keep night watch, and awake the tribe come morning with songs of bravery and virtue to greet the day with. Lamentors keep an eye out for tribe members who cannot pull their own weight, and prepare a mourning dirge to send them into exile with.

Competitiveness is the heart of goliath culture. Everything is a challenge, and goliaths will keep score at any task. Sports, games, and contests are a goliath's favorite way to spend time. They are driven to outdo each other and even themselves constantly, and believe strongly in a level and fair playing field. Goliaths can be extremely hard on themselves when they fail to live up to their prior victories, but defeat can be a powerful motivator to improve their skills. Few goliaths live to old age in the constant pursuit of surpassing their past accomplishments.

### Regions

##### The Nomadic Ones

A goliath tribe will set up a new temporary village every few months, moving from peak to peak. They will move to lower elevations during the dead of winter for ease of trading with settlements, then ascend the mountainous heights once more when the snow melts. The skywatcher will declare when they have overdrawn an area's resources, making the tribe move and follow new hunting trails.

##### Nomads of the Spine of the World

There are goliath tribes almost anywhere there are snowy peaks to live upon, and the Spine of the World is one of the more vast mountain ranges in Faerun. Far to the north, these near-impassable mountains mark the edge of the civilized world. With peaks over 20,000 feet high, life here is a constant test of one's strength of will. Tribes will sometimes send one of their own into the Ten Towns of Icewind Dale or to Mithral Hall for trading, and they hold the dwarves and their craftsmanship in high regard.

##### Leaving The Tribe

Some goliaths sent out on missions for the tribe never return, deciding to settle among other people and forge a new life. This may happen because they have fallen in love or become dissatisfied with their way of life. Exile is not uncommon and awaits any goliath who cannot pull their weight, either due to injury or age. Those exiled while young often find themselves signing up with the first adventuring or mercenary company they come across, their naturally imposing physique and survival-minded lifestyle making them adept for such lines of work. Many goliath sellswords are drawn to the Sword Coast.

### Appearance

**Kin of Stone:** Though their ancestral link to stone giants is far, far back in their past, their appearance yet bears the legacy. Most notably in their height, goliaths stand taller than the common races, and in the stone-gray shade of their skin. There is little difference between male and female goliaths in their height and weight. Though male goliaths are always bald, they can grow facial hair.

**Lithoderms:** A notable feature of goliath physiology are the bony growths studded throughout their skin called lithoderms, no bigger than pebbles. These have few nerve endings, and stand out quite prominently on their bodies. Some will even decorate their bodies by embedding small gems into their lithoderms.

**Markings of Fate:** Another distinctive feature of goliaths are the darker patches of skin across their bodies, often symmetrical and geometric in appearance. The shamans believe a goliath's fate is written in this skin mottling, and thus tattooing is culturally discouraged, tantamount to trying to change one's fate.

#### Varied Appearances

While all goliaths have tough stone-like skin and impressive height, they have some variation in appearance. Their eyes are bright blue, green, or all white, and can appear to glow faintly. Every goliath's skin mottling is a pattern unique to them.

### Faith

#### Skoraeus Stonebones

The god of stone giants often finds worship by goliaths, for their distant ancestral link. The Great Creator is a god of craftsmanship, urging his followers in the pursuit of art and lore. He is commonly depicted as a very tall stone giant with granite skin and thick corded muscle, with a chisel in one hand and a hammer in the other depicting his love of carving statues. Some Skoraeus-worshipping goliaths will leave their tribe in search of stone giants, hoping to learn and hone their craftsmanship from them.

#### Tempus, Lord of Battles

The worship of the god of warfare often appeals to goliaths, due to their love of competition. The Foehammer urges honorable combat and forbids cowardice, ideals that align closely to goliath culture. He is often depicted as a twelve foot tall human in battleworn armor, face hidden by his helm, and his body crisscrossed with scars and still-bleeding battle injuries. Many goliath exiles join his church, finding comfort in the battle-faith.

#### Silvanus, the Oak Father

The deity of wild nature, he is often revered by goliath druids, owing to the shamanistic culture of goliaths. He encourages a deep respect for nature among his followers, urging them to plant new trees and protect the forests. Many goliath hunter-gatherers will pray to Silvanus to grant them success before they go out. The Treefather is often depicted as an old, bearded face within a whorl of an especially large tree. Some goliath druids leave their tribe to aid others of the greenleaf faith.
