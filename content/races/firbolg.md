## Race Information

### Society

#### The Big Folk

Firbolgs are natural druids, an inborn tendency to such paths, and thus most firbolg chieftains were druids. They respected druidic wisdom, and strived to live in harmony with the land, being careful to never take more than they needed and to leave a place better than they found it. Their culture views forests as sacred places, becoming protective of them, and it is not uncommon to find firbolg rangers defending their woodland strongholds. They typically lived in simple homes made from fallen logs, almost never more than one-story. Their clans were small and made up of a few families, and would stick to a single forest they claimed as their own, though some were nomadic.

Their race was made tens of thousands of years ago when the goddess Othea, wife of the creator of true giants Annam All-Father, had an affair with the deity Ulutiu. From this affair sprung the giant-kin races, and the firbolgs were cast out of the giant kingdom of Ostoria once the secret came to light, though they held little grudge for this and many firbolg clans maintained good relationships with storm giants, who they still looked up to as the pinnacle of gianthood. Other firbolgs actively fought against the giant races, however, particularly those of evil alignment.

Rather than the ordning, the firbolg had their own code they lived by, with many clans requiring all firbolgs to carry a written copy at all times. While the origin has long been lost, the words have not. "Bravery, Effort, and Honor over birth. The tribe's honor over yours. The blood of the runt is the blood of a king. Give a thousand for nothing. Truth is the honor of the tribe." This code emphasized equality, honesty, charity, and consideration of the natural world before anything else. The majority of firbolgs tended to a lawful good alignment in their devotion to the protection of nature, but some outcasts were known to be more neutral or even fall to evil.

Seclusion suited firbolgs, they avoided outsiders and heavily distrusted humans. They were warily neutral to elves, druids, and most fey, but still preferred to keep to themselves more than anything else. When confronted with an intrusion, they would subtly try to divert them from their woodland strongholds or gently attempt to convince them to leave. If forced into conflict, however, firbolgs were mighty fighters with bear-like strength that rivalled their true giant cousins, trying to decimate their enemy in a single overwhelming attack.

### Regions

##### Adventurers

Few firbolgs become adventurers, a rare change from their withdrawn nature. Most firbolg adventurers are outcasts, thrown out from their clans for some offense taken such as the starting of a forest fire or the unnecessary killing of an animal. Others might be orphans, distanced from their heritage, or the sole survivors of a clan destroyed by some calamity. Some might be travelling as part of their druidic training, to gather wisdom for their tribe by stepping outside their comfort zone, or dedicated to fighting against evil-aligned true giants.

##### Feywilds

Many firbolg clans have made their home in the Feywilds, the vibrant environment drawing the giant-kin to it to serve as protectors of its forests. They have long lived in the realm of Faerie, though rarely interacting significantly with the fey Courts or eladrin cities. The firbolgs have come in conflict with their fellow giant-kin, the fomorians, often lending their powerful strength to defending the fey woodlands from the monstrous fomorians. Some firbolg clans have mingled with fey more than others, with fey-blooded firbolg becoming more common.

##### Moonshae Isles

The firbolgs of the isles diverged into a much more violent berserker-esque culture than the rest of their kin, owing to the imprisonment of their patron demigod Grond circa -5000 DR. These firbolgs fought frequently with the fomorians that also populated the isles, though were mostly overwhelmed and enslaved by their fellow giant-kin. In 1365 DR, they managed to free Grond, and their culture began a shift closer to the rest of their firbolg kin, though still more violent than typical.

### Appearance

##### The Giant-Kin

To say firbolgs are tall would be an understatement. They rival their true giant cousins in towering height, turning even the tallest humans into dwarves. Some are even as tall as the trees they protect, yet their footsteps hardly disturb the forest floor they walk upon, a lifetime of taking careful steps.

##### Strength

The average firbolg's strength is double that of a bear, and their appearance matches it, with every firbolg broad with visible corded muscle across their frames. They were quite heavy, usually weighing over 600 pounds, with a deep yet smooth voice to match. Their hands are proportionally larger, with thick fingers and fingernails, and their skin is far thicker and tougher than a human's. They have quite hairy bodies, thick and coarse hair all over them.

##### Faces of A Giant

Firbolgs are known for having bulging noses, proportionally larger than a human's would be, though eyes of normal size and human hues. Nearly every male firbolg sports a thick beard, though some trim it shorter to keep it manageable. Female firbolgs, in addition to having much more body hair than a human would, typically have thick hair worn long.

#### Varied Appearances

Firbolgs can have pinkish fleshy or blueish lurid skintones, a variation sometimes attributed to fey blood but common enough to be accepted as normal, with clans having firbolgs with both and seen as no different from each other. Some firbolgs have faun-like ears with fluffy fur growing from these ears, though others have more human-like ears. While they typically have red or blond hair, other hair colors are possible but more rare.

### Faith

#### Hiatea

The giant goddess of nature was much beloved by firbolgs, becoming akin to a patron deity for those who devoted themselves to protecting the forests. While wholly unrelated to the race, born of Annam and a sky goddess, she was raised among the firbolgs and still has a strong affinity for community and family. She expected all her priests to be capable of surviving in the wild, with her clergy divided between priests of their local communities and priests patrolling woodlands.

#### Diancastra

The trickster demigoddess of wit and pleasure among the giant pantheon, she is much like Hiatea being born of Annam and an unnamed mortal. She has a strong following among more fey-blooded or fey-aligned firbolgs for her wily nature, seeing her as an example to follow, though she has few true priests. Some revere her as a fertility goddess, too, though children born after praying to her in this aspect are known to be more reckless and chaotic.

#### Iallanis

The giant goddess of peace and mercy, her dogma of love and harmony and hope appeals well to firbolgs. Her priests work towards uniting all giant and giant-kin races, and she's taken over much of Annam's role as a fertility deity. With an open heart, she accepts any good-aligned creature, though Annam's policy on giant-kin meant they could not advance very far in giantish pantheon clergy.
