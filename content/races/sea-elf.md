## Race Information

### Society

#### The Water Elves

Sea elves first came to Toril around -25,400 DR, shortly after other elven subraces migrated to Toril from the Feywilds. They spent the first few thousand years of their time on Toril as nomads, traveling between the early elven kingdoms and the oceans. After the elven kingdoms became embroiled in war around -17,000 DR, this sent many sea elves and refugees into the oceans, with the refugees using a High Magic artifact to transform themselves into sea elves. Further Crown Wars in -11,000 and onward drove more refugees to the seas, with the sea elves doing their best to stay out of surface wars and grow their capital of Coryselmal.

The Inner Sea (or Sea of Fallen Stars) known to its inhabitants as Seros has seen much conflict between the aquatic races, resulting in twelve Seros Wars. The most recent was in 1369 DR, when the ancient megalodon Iakhovas and his forces of sahuagin and other evil aquatic races clashed with the sea elven city of Myth Nantar, with the sea elves supported by merfolk, shalarin, tritons, and even some morkoth. The megalodon lost when the High Mages banished Iakhovas to the Outer Sea. The prior Seros Wars saw the various aquatic races feuding for control over regions of the Inner Sea, with the First Seros War in -9839 DR initiated by sea elves against their sahuagin foes. Despite the wars coloring Serosian history, most aquatic races are open to working together and many cities have become a mix of populations.

Sea elves have little concept of private property. Everything they care about, they keep on them, everything else in sea elf settlements belongs to the community as a whole. Individuals typically have the privacy of their own homes, though they will freely share their hospitality if asked of them. Tools, weapons, and other practical items were all considered communally owned, and any sea elf could go grab the nearest one. Theft is barely a concept among sea elves, something that can cause problems for them on the surface. They have no blacksmithing and must trade for metal with surface races, so most of their tools and weapons are made of coral, bone, chitin, stone, shells, or narwhal horns. Gold was highly prized as it did not corrode easily underwater. They even had longbows with bowstrings made of a specialized kelp.

Among all the other aquatic races, sea elves hold dolphins in highest esteem, with most settlements having a pod of dolphins. Water genasi are also common, with sea elves taking in orphaned genasi children or water genasi who don't feel at home on the surface, and the sea elves have neutral relationships with merfolk. They have a more tense relationship with the shalarin, with sea elves previously trying to drive them out of the Inner Sea, and with tritons who dwell on the bottom of the ocean compared to the sea elven kingdoms in the shallows. The sea elves are ever in conflict with the sahuagin, often the target of sahuagin raiding parties, though sea elves are just as often proactive in fighting their greatest foe. Sahuagin have a subspecies called malenti, who look exactly like sea elves and would infiltrate sea elven society for their sahuagin clans, which furthers hatred between sahuagin and sea elves.

### Regions

##### Life Underwater

Sea elven settlements could typically be found in the shallows, areas of calm water in coral reefs or tall seaweed forests. These settlements are typically isolationist and self-sufficient, with loose feudal structures by a patriarchal nobility. Their settlements always have a tight-knit community, and are typically on the smaller size, sometimes even built along whale migration routes to interact with these magnificent beasts. Some young sea elves would even travel with a whale pod for a year as a pilgrimage. Many settlements would also have hippocampi and giant seahorses as mounts or pets.

##### Seros

Also known as the Inner Sea or Sea of Fallen Stars, in central Faerun. The Aryselmalyr Empire ruled over Seros for over 10,000 years, dominating much of Serosian culture, ending around -255 DR. Their capital Coryselmal was described as the grandest city of Seros, though it would eventually be destroyed and its grandeur outshone by Myth Nantar. After the Spellplague, the famed city of learning was partly exposed to the air and partly covered in coral, but still had a burgeoning population. Other sea elven kingdoms made their homes in the shallows, such as Naramyr and Selu'Maraar.

##### Trackless Sea

Sea elves had a city near Evermeet named Iumathiashae, described as a beautiful wonder, and was one of the many defenses for the isle of Evermeet. Another sea elven kingdom called Aluchambolsunvae was located north of Evermeet for the same reason. This underwater army was highly respected by the elves of Evermeet, and these sea elves settled around Evermeet tended to care about other elven subraces more than Serosian sea elves. Other smaller sea elven settlements dotted the Sword Coast, with many concentrated around Tethyr.

### Appearance

##### Like And Unlike Other Elves

Sea elves tend to be more robust and long-limbed than other elven subraces, adapting to a life of constant swimming. An average sea elf tends to be taller and heavier than the average high elf,with thicker skin to deal with cold temperatures, and their fingers and toes have thick webbing between them to better aid swimming. Their pointed ears look similar to other elves, though tend to be shorter and some have more ridges as an adaptation to hearing underwater. And of course, they have gills over their ribs and necks.

##### Seros vs Great Sea

The sea elves in these two regions tend towards different appearances. Serosian sea elves typically have shades of blue skin or silvery skin with white mottling over their body or white/silver patches. Great Sea aquatic elves tend towards varying shades of green skin, mottled and striped with brown.

Clothing: It's practical to dress lightly when living underwater, to reduce drag when swimming. Sea elves of all stripes typically wear little, and weave their clothes of underwater plants like kelp. Some may fashion hides or chitin of various aquatic creatures into armor, or trade with the surface for silk woven specifically for underwater. The tritons and shalarins of Seros fashioned silverweave armor of silver coral, lightweight but strong as chainmail, though it did not last above the water.

#### Varied Appearances

Sea elves have a wide variety of hair colors, across both Serosian and Great Sea elves: blue-green, emerald green, blue, black, silver, or even occasionally red. They similarly have various possible eye colors: turquoise, white, black, blue, green, and rarely silver.

### Faith

#### Deep Sashelas

A Seldarine deity and the patron of sea elves, aquatic elves also worship Corellon and the rest of the Seldarine. Every sea elf settlement has a temple to Deep Sashelas, often shaped like a giant shell. His clergy are known as Delphions, and he is also known as the Dolphin Prince, with dolphins common companions for his priests. The clergy promote cooperation between all aquatic races and fight to prevent sahuagin raids. Deep Sashelas' avatar is that of a sea elf with blue-green skin and eyes, and his symbol that of a dolphin leaping.

#### Istishia

The Lord of Elemental Water has a small but dedicated population of sea elven worshippers, for representing the mutable and ever-changing nature of water and for his neutral mindset. He is indifferent to his own followers, preoccupied with his own unpredictable thoughts. Those who travel to the Elemental Plane of Water, such as to the trade hub City of Glass, may be drawn to his worship. Many sailors also turn to his worship in hopes for safety on the waves as well, with some sea elves taking to sailing as a natural job for them.

#### Umberlee

Some evil sea elves, cast out of their culture for one reason or another, turn to the worship of the Sea Queen for her power. Others might be sailors, praying she doesn't drown them at sea. A city of sea elves refused to help the city of Northkeep in 400 DR, and were punished by the gods. This city, called Mareliar, turned to the worship of Umberlee. These evil sea elves are called marel, and are known for their spite and hatred of other races, eager in their worship of the Wavequeen.
