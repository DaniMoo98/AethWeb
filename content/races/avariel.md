## Race Information

### Society

Avariels wish for nothing more than to soar freely through the skies. Even their clothing reflects this desire, preferring loose, flowing garments like togas that do not snag upon their wings and makes flight both more enjoyable and aesthetically pleasing, as the fabric will ripple in the wind. Their love for flying often has them pitying their land bound kin, but Avariel understand that they are invaluable allies as they begin to leave their Aeries.

It is very common for Avariel to become clerics, as they favor a more philosophical existence that encourages spirituality. Given their hearts are old and their society is set in their ways, it is uncommon to meet an Avariel barbarian. While they may usually find purpose in a divine calling, they do not avoid or ban other pursuits, they simply prefer the divine over more martial or arcane paths. Now that they have deemed themselves safe, Avariel have begun to explore magic again.

Like all elves, Avariels revere art. Their largest Aerie stands as a testament to their mastery over glass, able to make it as strong as metal, thusly called glassteel. They have also perfected glassblowing, elevating it into a true art form. Even their armor is fashioned with brilliant glasteel. Their glassteel is both beautiful and deceptively strong despite its fragile appearance - a reflection of themselves.

Avariel society is divided into two sects - Warlike and Peaceful. During their youth, Avariels will spend time in each sect, learning their values and discovering where they are pulled towards. While these groups are separate in their beliefs and what they contribute to their people, they work together in perfect harmony. When an Avariel reaches maturity is usually when they decide what path they wish to pursue.

**Warlike Avariel** are those who found their calling wishing to defend their people, living by a strict and complex code of honor. To others, this code of conduct can seem extreme, as they consider it dishonorable to surrender, but also an insult to leave a defeated foe alive. Warlike avariel most commonly become fighters and rangers, preferring to use ranged attacks.

**Peaceful Avariel**, by contrast, devote themselves to scholarly pursuits. They hold art, religion, and knowledge in high regard. Divine and arcane casters typically settle into this sect. If pursuing wizardry, Avariels favor the schools of Conjuration and Transmutation, with Divination becoming more prominent as they seek out to unite with their fellow kin.

### Regions

##### Aerie-cities

Avariel prefer to live high atop high mountain ranges in reclusive, open settlements called Aeries or Aerie-cities. Several mountain ranges exist throughout Faerun, including the Star Mounts in the center of the High Forest, and the Sword Mountains to the east of the Mere of Dead Men. Because of how isolated and elevated these settlements are they cannot be easily, if at all, accessed by those who cannot fly. Although they loathe living underground or in enclosed spaces, as Avariel are prone to claustrophobia, they will sometimes carve personal homes into mountainsides, always ensuring that there are open balconies and overhangs that allow them free access to the sky. Aeries are usually built to blend urban planning with artistry, with sprawling terraces that overlook smaller peaks and structures, and several open plazas with sparkling fountains. A central plaza will usually be an Aerie's busiest location, with stalls set up during the day to accommodate trade, and is cleared for religious or other civic services. Buildings are tall and wide to accommodate their wings, with plenty of windows. Roofs are not often peaked, but are instead flat to act as an observation deck.

##### Aerie of the Snow Eagle

The Aerie of the Snow Eagle is the largest of all Avariel settlements, located just north of Rashemen on Mount Sundabar in the Icerim Mountains. Traveling Avariel discovered that the peak of the mountain was gone, leaving behind a vast plateau, and built upon the natural foundation to restore the missing peak with glassteeel. The Aerie is so secluded that when the Retreat came in 1344 DR, the Avariel within the Aerie could not hear it. At the Aerie's center is a temple dedicated to Aerdrie Faenya. Despite the Aerie's high elevation, the temple's main floor is lush with tropical life, warmed by sunlight that could easily pass through the crystal walls while locking in heat. Its last known leader, Aquilan Greatspan, has led the Avariel of the Aerie for the last five centuries as of 1367 DR.

##### Cambro Mountains on Gwynneth

Avariel can also be found in hidden Aeries along the peaks of the Cambro Mountains on Gwynneth, also called Sarifal, the largest island of the Moonshae Isles. The Avariel here have a deep devotion to Aurilandür, an aspect of Auril, rather than Aerdrie Faenya. The island itself is said to be lush with nature and breathtakingly beautiful, while the Cambro Mountains are large, granite pinnacles covered in glaciers.

### Appearance

**Winged:** Avariel are known for their soft, feathered wings, which can easily span up to sixteen feet in length. Their wings are a source of great pride, and appear in various colors including white, grey, brown, black and speckled patterns. Avariel spend a lot of time grooming their wings, believing them to be a blessing granted to them directly from Aerdrie Faenya.

**Graceful and nimble:** To accommodate flying with wings, all Avariel have hollow bones and longer torsos. Despite their lithe frames, they possess strong upper bodies, though their overall builds make them appear more delicate than other elves.

**Striking features and eyes:** Avariel are said to be the most beautiful of all elves with chiseled, angular features and large, expressive eyes. Their eye colors often range between striking shades of blue and green, and few Avariel have vivid violet eyes.

**Pale:** Most Avariel are rather pale in complexion, with tinges of silver or blue. Their hair is usually white or black, but it is not entirely uncommon to see Avariel with other hair colors.

### Faith

#### Aerdrie Faenya, the Queen of Avariel

Aerdrie Faenya is called many names, including the Lady of Air and Wind, and the Bringer of Rain and Storms. As the elven goddess of the sky, it is believed she blessed the Avariel with their wings, and is held in high regard above the rest of the Seldarine. Akadi is sometimes regarded as an aspect of Aerdrie, and is likewise respected among Avariel. Aerdrie is prone to whimsy, preferring to feel the air around her. This sense of unbound freedom means that she does not involve herself with the politics and culture of her fellow Seldarine, and is far more concerned with the survival of the remaining Avariel, though she still works closely alongside them. The clergy of Aerdrie Faenya dedicates themselves with exploration and maintaining good relations with good-aligned avian races.

#### Corellon, the Father of Elvenkind

Correlon bears many titles, including the Lord of Magic, the Poet of Night, and the Teacher of Arts. Each title is a testament to his preserving and governing all things held dear by the Tel'Quessir, including magic, music, and the arts. Fluid in form, he is both man and woman, and in his love for Araushnee opted to settle as a male warrior to balance her existence as a dark elven artist. Correlon is not only a decorated strategist but possesses the heart of a poet, musician and wizard. While he has no mercy for those who would strike against elves, he believes that mercy, love and pity are the three most important traits when dealing with those tempted by evil. It is believed that Correlon, alongside Sehanine Moonbow, guide Tel'Quessir souls towards their perfected state.

#### Angharradh, the Triune Goddess

Angharradh is the chief consort of Correlon and is believed to be the personification of three goddesses: Hanali Celanil, Sehanine Moonbow, and Aerdrie Faenya. She was born when the three joined together to heal Correlon during his battle with Araushnee, now known as Lolth. Within the Seldarine, she is second to Correlon, serving beside him as the Queen of Arvandor. Followers of Angharradh honor all three goddesses, offering prayers and observing whichever ceremonies and holy days matter to each.
