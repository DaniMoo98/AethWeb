## Race Information

### Society

Halflings are loyal humanoids that often form close knit communities. Many didn't follow kings or nobles. Instead they look to elders to aid them down the right path. Halflings value their traditions, passing them on from generation to generation.

Halflings are fond of stories and legends. They often enjoyed storytelling in taverns and at meals. Bread and cheese are often part of a Hin's diet, alongside Ale. Their food was often chewier and without strong spices.

Halflings, lightfoot and stout, are outgoing, friendly, and great at fitting into any community they find themselves in. Hin are often found in communities that are dominated by other races, fitting in amongst humans, dwarves, elves, or gnomes.

Halflings are sometimes said to be weaker than other humanoids, but this was not always true. Many are quick, dexterous with a strong courageous personality. They have quick reflexes and can recover from danger with their lucky streak. Halflings often took insults to their race with amusement. Their curious nature and unending wanderlust gave the average Hin plenty to keep them from boredom.

### Regions

##### Hin Community

Halflings form close knit communities, often settling within or on the outskirts of human dominated cities. Their cities are called "Burrows", structures that are above ground but also include tunnels across hills. Some Halflings preferred to be nomadic, migrating around every six months.

##### Hin from Luiren

A coastal kingdom south east of Faerun off The Great Sea, was the country of Luiren which was almost completely made up of Halfling folk. The capital city was the coastal city of Beluir and the kingdom was in between Dambrath and Estagund. The country was filled with ever growing green forests and wide lands. Halflings being masters of agriculture would cultivate the irrigated farmlands across the kingdom.

##### Hin and the Sword Coast

Many Halflings can be found among the vast cities lining the Sword Coast. Hin find opportunities within the bustling urban populations, adapting to the cultures around them with ease. They often enjoy large populations that thrive with their sociable nature alongside the many taverns to spread their tales. One would find a Halfling community within or on the outskirts of cities such as Baldur's Gate, Beregost, or Daggerford.

### Appearance

**Small Humans:** Halfings got their name for being roughly half the size of the average human with traits similar to humans. They are between 80-105 cm tall and weigh on average between 30-35 pounds. With a Rudy skin complexion, the average Halfling looked like a small human with the proportions of a typical human adult. Their hair and eyes are typically dark.

**Groomed:** Nearly all Halflings are incapable of growing a beard. Instead, they are often found sporting long sideburns, including female Hin which would only be a bit shorter. They wore complex hairstyles that included braids. Hin collected valuable items but often wore clothing that was comfortable and simple.

**Powerful Builds:** These beings may be small but they are mighty. Being dexterous in nature, they are able to move in and out of small places quickly and silently.

#### Varied Appearances

##### Lightfoot Halfling

These Hin are known as hairfeet or Luiren Halflings. Lightfoot's traits are similar to humans. They are most commonly seen with black or brown hair, hazel or brown eyes, and a ruddy skin complexion. Facial hair was rare among this race though men enjoyed sporting long sideburns or a beard and women sporting short sideburns. Lightfoots enjoy simple and well-made clothing. Comfortable and attractive garments favoring bright colors.

##### Stout Halfling

These Hin are also known as Strongheart Halflings or "Good Folk" by various races. Stout Halflings are shorter and stockier than others of their kin. Their traits are similar to humans with brown, blond, and black hair being common. Stout Halflings typically have blue or green eyes and enjoy wearing various kinds of armor. They are incapable of growing beards or mustaches but are known for their mid-cheek sideburns that both genders often braided.

### Faith

#### Yondalla, The Nurturing Matriarch

Yondalla is the creator of Halflings and the Matriarch of the Halfling Pantheon, Yondalla's Children. She has a charming and friendly personality alongside her love of curiosity, loyalty, and mischief. Yondalla favored life, fertility, and protection. She had bonds with various deities from other pantheons and scorned evil deities such as Bane, Cyric, and Talos.

#### Arvoreen, The Wary Sword

Arvoreen is the Halfling deity of defense, guardians, and vigilance. He was the closest to a god of war for the Hin and taught aggressive watchfulness of enemies but only reacting to aggression. It was dishonorable to attack first unless it was to stop evil practices. Thieving was okay if performed against a foe but extremely forbidden against other Hin or allies. Arvoreen is a member of Yondalla's Children. Stout Halflings often worshiped Arvoreen, relating to his teachings.

#### Brandobaris, Master of Stealth

Brandobaris is the halfling deity of thievery, stealth, and curiosity. He is a scheming and meddling god and a member of Yondalla's Children. Stout Halflings discouraged worship of Brandobaris. It was the lightfoot halflings who primarily shared his curiosity and grew up learning of his exploits. Brandobaris had a good relationship with Tymora, Garl Glittergold, and Erevan Ilesere and formed an informal group of mischief.
