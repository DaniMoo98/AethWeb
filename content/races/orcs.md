## Race Information

### Society

#### Through Rage and Bloodshed

An orcish tribe typically consists of a few hundred members. Any more would be a lot harder to sustain with the resources surrounding the Orcs lair. Other tribes are typically seen as rivals - contesting for the same resources. But there have been rare instances where different tribes came to work together. The tribe is led by a chieftain, orc bloodrangers - the champions of their people - often hold positions as low-ranking chiefs and bodyguards, the rest of the camp is divided into the lines of worship: The followers of Gruumsh and Luthic live in the best parts of the lair, while followers of Yurtrus are relegated to the farthest corners of the lair, far away from the rest of the tribe.

Orcs have booming birthrates to replenish the numbers of their lost warriors. The females who are about to give birth are relieved of any other duties and obligations and are being cared for by the followers of Luthic. Unlike other Races, who often bond with one partner in marriage for their life, orcs do not have mates. There are no pairs, no bondings, and generally males and females are often indifferent towards each other. Young Orcs also do not feel any parental love. They're raised to fight from a young age - turning them into deadly and experienced warriors once they've grown up.

Rage and Bloodshed are what makes the Orcs. They appreciate physical prowess and are indoctrinated into a life of violence from birth. Orcs have an instinctive love for battle - ingrained into them through both culture and character. Their favoured solution to any problem- Violence. And problems are easily found, for orcs are impatient and easily offended and angered.

The key to survival is savagery and force of numbers - at least for the orcs. A long standing strategy that so far has worked out for them. They have no interest in diplomacy and take what is needed by force. This is not just because of their character, but because they live in constant fear of their gods, who demand them to conquer. Orcs are very superstitious and even minor things are considered omens - the eerie whispering of the wind, a sudden change of the weather, everything is a sign of the gods that their shamans need to interpret. Over many centuries, different tribes have developed different, particular superstitions.

### Regions

##### Marauding Bands

The presence of Orcs was not one to be overseen, as wherever they made their home, marauding warbands would soon wander the lands, searching for their next prey. From settlements of the civilized races to travelling merchants - none were safe. Orcs mainly established their encampments in mountainous areas, around caves or in crevasses that offered natural protection. If neither of those were available, they would also fortify the ruins of dwarven and human settlements. While this was preferred, orcs were able to adapt and strive in every environment, staying in one area for as long as the surrounding resources sustained them.

##### Many-Arrows

The Many Arrows kingdom was founded under King Obuld - a Chosen of Gruumsh - about a century ago. Its territory was mostly near the cold terrain of the Spine of the World. It was ruled by kings and was known for being surprisingly civilized compared to other orcish tribes and settlements. The Many-Arrows held a tense peace with the other races of the Silver Marches, until the kingdom fell in the 15th century DR after the War of the Silver Marches. Even though the Kingdom was destroyed, the Many-Arrows tribe did still exist. Orcs healing from this area may be inclined to be more civilized.

##### Sword-Mountains

The sword mountains lie to the Northwest of Waterdeep. Given the landscape is the preferred settling-ground for the orcs, it is to no surprise that the Mountain-Range is inhabited by them. The Orcs living here are responsible for several conflicts with Waterdeep, which was close enough to be a target of their plundering warbands. Until the 10th century DR the steep craggy mountains were also inhabited by trolls - until the orcs banded together and forced them out. Orcs that hail from this area are inclined to follow the bloodthirsty, marauding ways of Gruumsh.

### Appearance

**Hulking: **Most orcs were over 6 feet tall and heavy. With a stooped build, broad shoulders and a muscular physique, they loomed over many of the civilized folks of Faerun.

**Bestial:** When angered, they perhaps could be compared to a beast. But this is not just the case for their behavior. Their look also matches the description - with a face of porkine appearance, wolflike tipped ears and of course large lower canines that are akin to a boar's tusks.

**Distinct Features: **Other than their tusked mouths, Orcs also had small beady-eyes, often of a reddish colour, gray skin and black coarse hair. Their low foreheads also contributed to a very different appearance compared to other humanoid races.

#### Varied Appearances

**Young and Dangerous: **The orcs seen most often were the warbands that ventured out of their encampments and caves. Often composed of orcs that were still in their prime. They were considered old in their mid-20s, venerable in their mid-30s. Due to their warlike nature most never aged beyond this.

**Underestimated: **Women and the elderly were often underestimated. They cared for and raised the young, often staying at the camps and caves. Those of them that followed Luthic also grew their nails to sharpened claws that were painted black.

### Faith

#### Gruumsh, He who watches

The ruler of the orc pantheon. He mainly sees his orcs as an instrument of revenge against the civilized races. Among the orcs it is believed that Gruumsh can not be chosen. He is the one that chooses. And only those that prove themselves through skill in war and ferocity are considered a good worshipper and worthy of his favour. The Head of the orcish pantheon is violent and bloodthirsty. His wrath is easy to spark and once it is ignited, it undoubtedly clouds his judgment. The core aspect of his worship is to weed out the weak and nurture strength. He has an ancient vendetta against Corellon for it is said he took Gruumsh's eye. His allies lie mainly within his own pantheon: 'The tribe of he who watches'.

#### Luthic, The Cave-Mother

She is Gruumshs wife and considered the presence that binds the orcs together. As the patron of female orcs, healing and home, her followers often tend to the brood, raising them to be strong and cruel. She is considered the most matronly and least wicked of the orc pantheon and often underestimated, for she is quite cunning and wise, capable of manipulating others to serve her needs. She is one of the few individuals that is able to even influence Gruumsh and through him, get her will.

#### Yurtrus, The Lord of Maggots

Also known as the white hand - due to his oddly pristine white hands despite his rotting flesh and a body consumed by corruption, Yurtus is the god of death and disease. He is the embodiment of 'anti-life' and was often more dreaded and appeased than revered by his followers, who themselves were often outcasts that had joined his cult. Even by orcish standards he was considered unpleasant and uncommunicative. Taking the role as a punisher within his pantheon, his omens were usually the outbreak of disease and rot and the tribes who befell it would know they had angered their gods and strayed too far from Gruumshs path of war. Like the rest of the pantheon, he hates the dwarf, elven and goblonoid gods.
