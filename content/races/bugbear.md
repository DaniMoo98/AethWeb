## Race Information

### Society

##### Sloth

Nothing softens the fist or loosens the lips of a bugbear quite like sleep, the promise of rest on a bed made for a king may well be the greatest chip in an ongoing bargain. A great many hobgoblins scoff and sigh when the formal planning for their raid includes time for naps, of course the bugbears demand such a rest for their aid and they are rarely refused.

##### Greed

Very few goblinoids are exempt from very deep seated greed. They all lust after the biggest seat at the dinner table, the shiniest dagger in the latest haul, and of course the juiciest leg of whatever finds itself hung over their fires. Though few go to the lengths dragons do it would not be a truly unheard of sight for a bugbear to rest atop their newest find, if nothing else but to ensure another doesn't nick it while they sleep.

##### Bribery

Most bugbears are content to eat and sleep their days away, perhaps the odd hobby takes a sliver of that time or maybe an upcoming raid has piqued their interests. For the large majority if you need their help a deal must be struck, your goods for their time. Feeding a bugbear is often the easiest option, make them a feast and that will tilt the scales in your favor though such a thing is fleeting and heavier goods should always be prepared as well.

##### Unpredictable Nature

Many facets of goblinoid life are self centered or actively malicious, while that certainly applies to bugbears it's not exactly the best descriptor. "When it suits them" is perhaps the easiest way to define a bugbears general personality. They enjoy a good fight and certainly are capable though would likely refuse if they simply didn't want to that day, this extends to situations of importance as well. Even in the most dire of circumstances a bugbears whim may alter its course entirely.

### Regions

##### Versatile Nomads

Bugbears like most goblin kind could live just about anywhere in Toril, they are unbothered by the sun as it warms rocks to lay on, and the frigid north cannot cut through their natural fur coats. Every militant encampment a hobgoblin raises will surely have a sleeping quarters large enough for a bugbear and every goblin cave will soon find space for their larger cousins should one find the urge to nap.

##### Undermountain

The famous dungeon below Waterdeep is home to several bugbear enclaves, including those belonging to the Xanathar Thieves' Guild and Azrok's Hold. Through the warlord Azrok's recent failings a great many of his followers have fled his domain seeking either work in the thieves guild or traveling further to make a life in the nearby area.

##### Abundant but stubborn skill

Though it's not easy to get a bugbear to work they can be motivated by a great many things. Should you find proper compensation and a particularly reasonable bugbear they could make for excellent smiths and forge hands, their skill with a blade and unnerving finesse makes them adept butchers assuming you don't mind a touch of hair in your food.

### Appearance

**Tall:** Many would be shocked to find bubears are rather tall beings, despite being lumped in with goblin kind they are towering figures often standing whole heads taller than orcs and hobgoblins. This size helps further add to the shock when one sneaks up upon a group of unsuspecting adventurers.

**Hairy:** Though accounts vary, many bugbears are described as being nearly head to toe covered in fur. Many of them can appear far closer to a particularly hairy hobgoblin and to the untrained eye some could look similar to a rather lazy ape. This shaggy unkempt appearance helps the bugbear blend into its typical environments making for easier ambushes and an easier time to find a napping spot.

**Shabby Attire:** Very few goblinoids have the means to collect pristine garb and fewer still would care to. The average clothing, if it could be called such a thing, for a bugbear would likely consist of little more than trousers with pockets for holding snacks and a bag tied with rope for knives. When the call to war is sounded they don makeshift armor, enough to stop a blow or two but always seeking to keep their movement fluid and their footsteps silent.

**Hygiene:** Though few would be shocked to find that bugbears don't value cleanliness or other similar standards they don't actively seek to break them. A keen sense of smell keeps bugbears at very least doused in water from time to time in an attempt to ensure their tracking skills perform well.

### Faith

#### Hruggek

Once the chief deity of bugbears Hruggek exhibits violence, combat ability, and the skill needed to plan and execute ambushes. Such traits are often embodied by bugbears far and wide, once savagery has been committed in their god's name the defeated parties heads are taken as a dark offering to Hruggek.

#### Maglubiyet

From his throne of flaming iron, Maglubiyet commanded the souls of goblinoids and worgs to wage eternal war against the orcish petitioners of Gruumsh. Through his devoted "Sons of Maglubiyet" the conquering god seeks to further his reach via war and bloodshed.

#### Bane

Though Bane commands a great many followers who have taken to much more civil and seemingly understandable fronts the goblinoids under his command are anything but. Violent disputes, rampant bloodshed, and control through aggression are all key features to the Black Lord's rituals.
