This creature has an alternative physical visage that they can wear. They have ONE polymorph form that may be changed on a monthly basis through a ticket. This polymorph MUST be in the form of a woman of adult age or older.

**Aquatic**
Gain access to the following benefits while the Swimming status is active:

- 15% vulnerability to Lightning damage, stacking with other sources of vulnerability

- 50% resistance to Fire damage, does not stack with other sources of Fire resistance

- +1 Initiative while swimming

- +1 to tracking rolls when tracking through major waterways

- No Attack penalty while swimming

- No reduction to Somatic Components for spellcasting

- No Movement penalty while swimming, regardless of armor class

**Natural Weapon**
You may use Natural Weapon attacks with your feral claws to deal 1d4+STR or DEX hitpoints of damage. These claws do not carry any additional bonuses such as keen, reach, etc.

**Weird Magic**
The magic of hags is inscrutable, strange and utterly unpredictable, fostered over a lifetime of wicked experimentation unique to every hag. You gain access to every ritual at their appropriate levels, using the spellcasting modifier appropriate to your class.

**Fluff Spells**
This creature has access to the fluff spells Minor Illusion, Prestidigitation, & Mage Hand.

## Race Information

### Society

#### The Wicked Ones

Many mortals know of hags as mischievous bargaining creatures. The most ancient hags can even form warlock pacts. Other hags will cast spells on a mortal's behalf, divining their fate or cursing some enemy. They are also creatures of gossip, secrets, and lore, all of which they will sell or buy in a shadowy deal. These bargains always have a dark side, some benefit gained for the hag, such as trading something or corrupting the person closer to evil. Hags prey on those desperate enough to come to them for their magical powers, finding these the most amusing. A hag will always have customers, those clever ones who think they can outsmart her, but it rarely ends well for them.

Many types of hags exist, each as horrible and wretched as the last, from the fiendish night hag to the wintry bheur hags. But most common in the region of the Lost Hills are green and sea hags. A green hag thrives on creating tragedy in particular, and they are the most tolerant and sociable of all the hag types, though this simultaneously makes them the hags most determined to terrorize and destroy society. Sea hags are more reclusive and brooding, hating all things beautiful the most out of all the different kinds of hags, driven to turn beauty into dread and misery.

All hags feel a dark sisterhood with each other, even when they can barely stand each other. Sometimes they will band together into a coven to pool resources, almost always in an uneven number so disputes have a tiebreaker. Hags often surround themselves with all sorts of underlings. Servants, apprentices, undead, bodyguards, indebted fey, and of course hexbloods. Humanoids transformed with a portion of the hag's power, tied to them, turning them fey. Hexbloods are one of many ways hags reproduce, with some hexbloods so driven to despair or hungry for power they end up submitting to a ritual that turns them into hags.

Hags are innately creatures of magic, and know secrets of magic long since lost to wizarding traditions. This knowledge gives them an unmatched arrogance, leading to hags believing their magic is better than what the gods bestow, they see becoming a god's cleric as a humiliating position. She might play a servitor role of advisor or court mage, but they are always looking for an opportunity to benefit themselves or scheming a treacherous plot. This weird magic extends to their reproduction. They might swallow a human child and spit her up, beginning a decades-long transformation into a hag, or mate with a human male and leave the child to him to grow until the time is right, or have stranger origins still.

### Regions

##### Hag Lairs

A hag typically lairs somewhere close enough to civilization for occasional visitors, but far away enough for her desired seclusion and to not be seen as an immediate threat. It always reflects her evil and chaos in some manner, more often in small ways for experienced hags. A sea hag might lair in a shipwreck, or somewhere underwater or coastal. A green hag may lair in a hollowed out giant tree, preferring swamps or forests. They typically have a secret exit or even two or three, and some may have an unassuming front to fool visitors.

##### Feywilds

Hags are fey creatures, native to Faerie, much to the dismay of all other fey. Murkendraw, a gloomy and dismal demesne in the Feywilds, is a bloated and wretched swamp the size of the sea. Many hags naturally make their home here, comfortable among the skiff-sized feymire crocodiles and carnivorous plants, honing their magical skills and feuding with each other. Some hags might find solitude in ruined Cendriane, or sell their services to the fomorian rulers of the Feydark.

##### Moonshae Isles

An archipelago in the Sea of Swords, it is a realm heavily connected to the fey. An eladrin kingdom called Sarifal rules over much of the island Gwynneth, and many fey are known to cavort across all the Moonshae Islands. Hags particularly enjoy tricking their fellow fey, leading many hags to lair in the swamps and on the coasts of the islands. Such a promising location leads to competition between fellow hags, which rarely ends up well for anyone involved.

### Appearance

**Wicked Witches: **Hags' bodies were mostly human in shape, yet they had long since twisted into the form of monstrous crones. Their skin hung in wrinkled, withered folds, a patchwork of blotches, warts, and darkened moles that spoke of ancient wickedness. Their long, spindly fingers ended in talon-like nails, hard as steel and sharp as razors, while their mouths were lined with jagged, blackened teeth and breathed out a foul, noxious stench.

Their bodies were treated with the same grotesque care. Many sharpened their teeth, picked at wounds to keep them raw and oozing, and exaggerated their natural deformities with almost loving attention. For hags, cultivating ugliness was an art of its own.

#### Varied Appearances

Though all hags shared a monstrous, hideous nature, their subraces and the environments they inhabited left distinct marks on their physical features.

##### Green Hags

Green hags dwelled near rivers, swamps, or forest edges, tied closely to the natural world they delighted in corrupting. Their skin bore a greenish hue and a texture reminiscent of rough bark, and they often adorned themselves and their lairs with mud, moss, and forest weeds. When using disguises, green hags favored assuming beautiful forms, weaving lies behind borrowed faces to ensnare the unwary with deceptive charm.

##### Sea Hags

Sea hags lived in the deepest waters, farther than most hags dared to go. Their blue or gray skin reeked of rotten fish, seashells clung to their bodies, their hair hung like wet moss, and many had gills or finlike features. Their cold, blank eyes resembled those of a fish. Like other hags, they reveled in deception, but they were fiercely territorial. They lured sailors by appearing as helpless women, drawing victims toward the waves. Even among their monstrous kin, sea hags were known for extreme ugliness; something they proudly displayed, even in the forms they shapeshifted into.

### Faith

#### Themselves

It is rare to hear of a hag becoming a cleric. They may begrudge a warlock patron long enough to siphon some power from them, but subservience to a god is something they can barely stand. Certainly not with enough sincerity for a god to bestow clerical power on them. Hags believe their weird magic is better than anything the gods can give them. Sometimes they're even arrogant enough to set up cults to themselves, tricking people into following them to satiate their ego.

#### Archfey

Hags would sometimes begrudgingly serve an archfey, as a warlock or courtier, but it would always be in service to their own ends. They viewed other fey with something approximating respect, something they bestowed on no other creature. The Queen of Air and Darkness, ruler of the Unseelie Court, counted several hags among her followers, particularly those who had a vengeance against members of the Seelie Court. Other hags pay homage to Baba Yaga, The Mother of all Witches, or her surrogate daughter Zybilna the Witch Queen of Prismeer.

#### Dagon

While not a true god capable of granting clerical power, many sea hags worship this Lord of The Darkened Depths. An obyrith so ancient he existed before even the primordials came to Toril, he has long since been imprisoned in the Trench of Lopok in the Sea of Fallen Stars by the tritons. His followers are ever scheming on how to free him and his fellow obyrith lords from their drowned prison, and the demon lord's reach grows more and more, barely impeded by the Trench.
