## Race Information

### Society

#### The Worth of Stone

More than any other race that has walked the world of Faerun, the dwarves know well the worth of stone. That of which mountains, by which the cliffs that stop the seas are forged, and that within which they make their homes. The dwarves are known for their fortress cities, often called Citadels or Dwarfholds, which reach deep into the earth and high into the skies, dominating their local landscapes. It is from these primordial cities that virtually all modern dwarves descend.

Dwarves imitate stone in the way they have shaped their society, rigid and often unmoving, with a fixation on a respect for those who have come before, those stones that have laid the foundation for what is. In this sense, dwarven society is, more than almost any other, downward branching system of familial support structures where the oldest and most talented guide future generations in trade and war. These extended structure family units are often known as clans, and can encompass dozens of smaller families united by a council of elders who share a final last name. Not everyone within a clan is related by blood, but often they share a home and a history for which all are proud to be born into, or join through marriage.

Dwarves are known for their self righteousness, often utterly set in their ways, even the most far flung dwarf is likely to adhere to the belief in their old gods, even if they do not directly worship them. Rarely will dwarves betray the interests of their people, and rarer yet will they forgive ancestral enemies or abandon promises made. This stubborn dedication to honesty and the strength of one's word, the unmoving Worth of Stone, is what defines in the utmost the mentality of a dwarf regardless of clan, culture, or homeland.

The dwarf themselves were usually divided into two primary groups. The Shield Dwarves of Northwestern Faerun, and the Gold Dwarves of Southern Faerûn. While culturally similar, the Shield Dwarves had a history that was more marked by struggle, difficulty, and an intermingling with other races for survival, the Gold Dwarves had seen fewer setbacks and existed as a thriving series of nations and states, more independent and self sustaining than their far flung northern kin.

### Regions

##### The Stout Kin

Dwarves were a far spread race; but often preferred to live in semi-subterranean strongholds nestled between the surface and the highest reaches of the upper dark. Unlike the natural caverns that made up the underdark, dwarven tunnel networks were carved by their own hands, often leading to vast, intricate, and ever expanding networks of mines and passages that expanded from their centralized communities. Some of these networks could host as few as a few dozen dwarves, while the largest of these could host tens of thousands of kin.

##### Stout Kin of the Sword Coast

Often called mountain or Shield Dwarves, Northern Faerun and the Sword Coast were home to the second largest concentrations of the dwarven population in the world. These were the dwarven survivors of the fallen kingdom of Delzoun, which reigned in the region until roughly the year -100, where its remains fragmented into the various clans and Dwarfholds of the modern era, the main strongholds of the north. Those dwarves who do not live in major or minor strongholds likely live in the great cities of the sword coast, places such as Baldur's Gate, Waterdeep, and Neverwinter. These northern dwarves are generally referred to as the Mountain Dwarves, for they burrow deep.

##### Stout Kin of the Great Rift

The lands far to the south are host to the Great Rift, the world's largest canyon network-- centered upon many trade routes, and with innumerable riches and dwarves found within. Here one can find the Gold Dwarves of the South, who have known less adversity, turmoil, and woe than their northern counterparts. These dwarves, even in their darkest of times, enjoyed a golden age that dozens of dwarven generations, before the Great Spellplague saw half of their kingdom falling into the underdark. Yet with the onset of the second sundering, the dwarven gods have rebuilt their favorite lands, and replenished this once struggling kingdom to its former glory. Adventurers set forth in droves to explore and seek greatness, ever curious, confident, and prideful of the works of their people. These southern dwarves are generally referred to as the Hill Dwarves by their kin, but are known as gold dwarves to all, for they do not burrow deep.

### Appearance

**Stout:** Dwarves are infamously stout people; standing between four and five feet in height, but weighing more than a fullgrown man or woman. Regardless of gender, dwarves are dense beings.

**Groomed:** Almost all dwarves take immaculate care of their hair, be they bearded or not. Dense braids, golden fixtures and decorations, and routine maintenance ensure cleanliness. While not common, it is not unheard of for a dwarven woman to grow a beard, or a dwarven man to lack one.

**Powerful Builds:** Regardless of gender or even age, dwarves sport powerful builds and immense reserves of strength and endurance. Dwarves routinely survive blows that would kill other good folk, or produce feats of strength only the greatest of men or elves could hope to reproduce.

#### Varied Appearances

##### Mountain Dwarves

Are known to be pale or even greyish in skin hues, lightly colored hairs in red and blonde hue, though darker tones surely exist.

##### Gold Dwarves

Are known to be brown or tanned in the skin hues, and sport darkly toned beards in browns and blacks.

### Faith

#### Moradin, the Father in Stone

Moradin is, barring the possible exception of his long-time ally Corellon and the Overgod Ao, is among the strongest of deities in all reality. The lawful good lord of dwarves is known by my titles. The All-Father, The Dwarffather, The Creator, and the Soul-Forger. It was his will that brought his people to the surface, and it is his will that has kept dwarves honorable and loyal to their oaths, their paths in life, and their kin. Striking the balance between pushing his worshippers to strive for excellence without drowning them in cruel expectations, Moradin is and shall always be, the greatest of the gods of mortal folk.

#### Berronar Truesilver, The Revered Mother

Berronar is, without fail, the loving mother to Moradin's kindly fatherhood. Known for her fine intermingling of mercy and kindness towards kin and strangers, and her warrior's pragmatism and determination. Known often as Matron of Hearth and Home, it is she who tends to the lands of the dwarves, and seeks to ensure the safety of kinholds and fortresses, be they large or small. Ever forgiving to her children, and ever pushing for peace amongst dwarvenkind, something that extended even to the Fallen Duergar.

#### Dumathoin, The Silent Keeper

Dumathoin is a distant and strange god, even to the dwarves. As the Keeper of Secrets under the Mountain, Dumathoin is associated with all treasures and horrors hidden below the land, and offers caution to often greedy dwarves, or generous discovery to those dwarves who are most in need of such windfall. As the guardian of those things that are buried, secrets that outlive their makers, and the buried dead-- Dumathorin is at a strange intersection between being deity of secreted knowledge, and a guardian of the lost dead.
