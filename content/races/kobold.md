## Race Information

### Society

#### The Little Dragons

None can deny the ties kobolds have to dragons. This relation is a source of great pride for them, with legends of their draconic origins passed down from elders to hatchlings imparting great awe for all things chromatic dragon. Kobolds view it an honor to serve a chromatic dragon, and may even attempt to serve one in secret if the chromatic dragon refuses to accept them. A kobold warren near a dragon lair will offer regular sacrifices to the dragon, and kobolds in general view dragons as the embodiment of glory, cultural heroes or demigods. Many kobolds are sorcerers, an arcane blessing they feel an aspect of this connection to dragons, and arcane magic takes on an almost religious significance amongst kobolds.

A typical kobold tribe is gerontocratic--the oldest kobolds lead, often a sorcerer looked up to for their arcane magic and for having evaded death so far. Everything in a kobold tribe's day-to-day life is centered on survival, and tribes are often communal due to frequently being overcrowded. Eggs are cared for in a central nursery by experienced caretakers, and hatchlings are self-sufficient at birth. Kobolds have simultaneously among the highest birth and death rates among intelligent species, and believe if they die in service to the tribe, they will be reincarnated immediately into an egg. Kobolds view their other tribemembers as one extended family, though can tell who they are biologically related to with subtle scents.

One can expect to find an almost dizzying number of traps within an average kobold lair. Trapmaking is considered the greatest art form for kobolds, with clever and inventive traps, and one-upmanship almost a sport among kobolds. They'll use anything for traps, even scavenged weapons and armor. The typical trap is a clay pot or ceramic bowl stuffed with vermin or alchemical substances, covered in dirt in a passageway or readied to be dropped from above. Their skill in craftsmanship extends to jewelrymaking, kobolds love making jewelry of any shiny things they collect and are quite proud of their colorful amalgamations. Some kobolds also take to making wind or percussion instruments, even making innovative messaging systems through song.

From a young age, every kobold is taught to hate gnomes, and the feeling is mutual. The two races barely manage civility toward each other even under optimal conditions. If gnomes and kobolds are ever in proximity to one another without fighting, it's only because each side is taking the time to plan a decisive assault. This hatred extends to fey for their pranks, likening them to gnomes. This hatred stems from the god of gnomes, Garl Glittergold, trapping the kobold god Kurtulmak in an endless maze for all eternity, a prank played as the gnome god escaped with a piece of treasure from Tiamat's horde Kurtulmak was trying to retrieve.

### Regions

##### Warren Life

Kobold warrens are typically underground, a mined series of caves, with mining one of the most common professions. Some will settle in other dark and cramped places like overgrown forests or swamps, though. Their warriors will patrol in a 10 mile radius around their warren, attacking anything they find for food. At least one section of virtually every kobold tunnel is rigged to collapse, enabling a tribe to quickly and easily seal off a passage in the event of incoming danger. Most kobold lairs are located wherever they find natural veins of metals or gems, and are lightly decorated, with most kobolds keeping all their possessions on them at all times.

##### Undermountain

An enormous dungeon in Mount Waterdeep created by the mad mage Halaster Blackcloak, over time, some kobold tribes have laid claim to various chambers or layers in the vast and magical Undermountain. These winding halls even connect to the sewer system of Waterdeep, and the deepest are miles underground. The lure of treasure in the dungeon complex and rumors of dragons have drawn more than a few kobolds to the dangerous locale. Kobolds living here have their survival skills tested to the max constantly, with Undermountain termed the largest known mass grave of adventurers in all of Faerun.

##### Shipwreck's Coast

A peninsula along central Sword Coast known for the graveyard of wrecked ships, and for the dangers here that prey on folk upon the Coast Way. In between Baldur's Gate and Amn, the road that passes this cursed coast sees a constant stream of trade and travelers. Small bands of kobold tribes have made their home here, some even fashioning crude lairs out of the shipwrecks, all to swarm the caravans passing by. They compete with ogres, trolls, goblinoids, and bandits for the prize, however. The town of Beregost lies nearby, as well as a recent Amnian fortified waystation intent on dealing with all the threats.

### Appearance

##### Reptilian

Notably in contrast to their claimed kin of dragons, kobolds are cold-blooded and rely on their environment for warmth. They appear like short lizards, with sinewy double-jointed and naturally bent legs, their bodies physically weak but agile and built for speed. They have a short and thick scaled tail, akin to a common lizard. Their face is akin to a crocodile's, with a long snout and a dog-like black nose at the end. Many of the characteristics of a kobold's head display rudimentary features of dragonkind: forward-facing nostrils, ear holes, a forked tongue, and short, keratinous horns from the top of the skull.

##### Horns

Every kobold has at least one pair of short, almost stubby horns. Some may have more than one pair, or smaller spikes all over their head, a feature they proudly claim represents their link to dragons. These horns are typically colored tan or white, though some might be black, and rarely longer than the length of a human hand.

##### Shedding

Like other reptiles, kobolds shed their skins, though not all in one piece at one time, it's shed in patches. This process takes up to a week if allowed to transpire naturally, but kobolds accelerate this by scrubbing the old skin from their bodies. Shedding happens from once a month to about once a season. They often use oil to keep their scales shiny in between cycles. Kobolds are meticulous about their teeth and claws, as well. A flat stone in every kobold dwelling serves as a tool for polishing claws, the sharper the better.

#### Varied Appearances

Most kobolds have reddish scales, but that is far from the only color they can be. As well as scale colors matching their chromatic dragon kin (green, black, white, blue), they can also have tan, brown, gray, and orange scales. Most kobolds in a tribe will be of a similar color, though if two tribes merge, the result will be a spread of both colors in the population.

### Faith

#### Kurtulmak

The patron deity of kobolds, who hates all other life besides his kin. He is variously called Tiamat's child, or the dragonspawn of a long-ago green dragon Caesinsjach ascended to godhood by the dragon god Asgorath. Now forever trapped by the gnome god Garl Glitterbone, his hatred for all things gnomes and pranks runs through every kobold. No kobold warren is complete without a temple to their god, sometimes two for good measure. He preaches to his children to win battles by any means necessary, to retreat if it is the only way to live, and to mete out revenge on every enemy, regardless of how long that retribution takes to achieve.

#### Tiamat

The five-headed goddess of chromatic dragons, said to be the mother of Kurtulmak in some legends. Some kobold tribes, particularly those who serve a dragon, will worship her and set up a shrine or temple to her in their lair. The Dragon Queen desires to take control over the Realms, and dispatched to her clerics the twin goals of amassing an ever-greater hoard for her and to sabotage other faiths, especially those of her enemies like Bahamut and Bane.

#### Asmodeus

Some kobolds will break away from the draconic-centered worship of their society, though they will often still end up worshipping a lawful evil overlord such as Asmodeus. Both Kurtulmak and Tiamat have fiendish ties as well, and some kobolds turn to his worship in hopes of getting arcane power through a warlock pact, such as if they did not inherit sorcerous powers and are envious of those in their tribes who have. The Lord of the Nine Hells finds it easy to collect such followers.
