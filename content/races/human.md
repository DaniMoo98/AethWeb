## Race Information

Known for their adaptability, ambition, and versatility, humans have made significant impacts on the course of history and the shaping of the realm.

### Society

Humans in Faerûn exhibit immense diversity, shaping the continent's history with their adaptability, ambition, and versatility. They establish settlements in various environments, from the teeming cities like Waterdeep and Baldur's Gate to the rugged frontiers of the North. Human societies in Faerûn vary greatly, showcasing a range of governments and cultures. They build some of the most complex and populous settlements, including kingdoms and city-states. In their professional pursuits, humans display remarkable adaptability, mastering a vast array of trades and crafts.

#### Regions

Humans are widespread across Faerûn, inhabiting various regions, each with its unique cultural and societal traits. Notable regions include:

##### Cormyr:

A kingdom known for its chivalry and strong military.

##### Waterdeep:

A bustling port city and a hub of trade and politics.

##### Calimshan:

An ancient and wealthy empire rich in exotic cultures.

##### Neverwinter:

A city famed for its beauty and skilled craftsmen.

##### Amn:

A nation driven by commerce and mercantile interests.

##### Baldur's Gate:

A diverse city at the heart of trade and commerce.

##### Tethyr:

Known for its forests, plains, and ancient history.

Humans' adaptability and ambition have allowed them to establish diverse communities and play pivotal roles in Faerûn's ongoing narrative.

### Relations

Humans have a unique ability to coexist with many races. They often serve as the connecting link between diverse races, with mixed-race individuals like half-elves and half-orcs commonly having human ancestry. This adaptive and open-minded nature of humans fosters alliances and cooperation across Faerûn's varied races.

## Civilization and Culture

Humans have a relatively shorter history compared to other races in Faerûn but have significantly influenced the realm's course. They have founded empires, cities, and kingdoms, shaping the political and cultural landscape of Faerûn. Their history is marked by both cooperation and conflict with other races, showcasing their dynamic and transformative role in the continent's history.

### Religions

Humans in Faerûn worship a variety of gods from the Faerûnian pantheon. Deities such as Helm, Lathander, Mystra, and Torm are revered, each representing different aspects of life, morality, and the cosmos. The diversity in religious belief is a reflection of the varied human cultures across the continent.
