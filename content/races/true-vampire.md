## Race Information

### Society

#### The Unliving Ones

The curse of undeath warps and twists a vampire's emotions, withering or corrupting what attachments they had in life. Even positive feelings become dark mirrors, love may become obsessiveness, protectiveness might turn into controlling behaviors. As the years go on, even these foul emotions turn numb. Ancient vampires go to terrifying lengths to feel a glimpse of their mortal emotions, and often try to replace them with possessions, collecting trophies (some of them even living) to sate their twisted desires. Rumors swirl about the origin of this wretched curse that has existed since time immemorial, assigning blame to Orcus, some long dead evil god, or the Shadowfell itself. None know for certain, not even the oldest and most learned vampires.

A vampire is bound to their coffin, as their soul's anchor to the Material Plane. When their body is destroyed, they will turn to a gaseous form and escape back to wherever their coffin is, squeezing through even the smallest cracks. From here, they will painfully regenerate their body, weakened throughout this process. While in this state, they can be permanently killed with a stake through their foul heart. Due to this, they will go to any length to secure their coffin's location, anything from arcane wards to mazelike lairs to more.

A vampire will spread their curse for any number of reasons. Perhaps a twisted desire for having 'children', wanting to give a lover immortal life, desiring a sturdy minion, to torment someone, or simply to amuse themselves. It is rare for them to make their fledgeling into a true vampire, their dark nature desiring no equal to themselves. More often, a fledgeling will engineer some plot to kill their sire so they can ascend, or injure them enough they can drink their blood. True vampires rarely get along as well, natural rivals over the same source of food, though some vampires are drawn to collecting lesser undead to control as some wretched imitation of a kingdom.

Their thirst for blood is everpresent, though tempered and faint for a few days after feeding. Being starved of blood will not kill a vampire, but after long enough without sating their gnawing hunger, they start to frenzy. Driven mad by their bloodthirst, they will go to almost any length to feed, even disrupting their anonymity or long-spun plans. Some vampires subsist on animal blood or imitation blood made by alchemy (called bloodwine), but find it inferior to humanoid blood. Some vampires become quite picky, preferring the blood of a specific gender or race or lifestyle, each one a different taste to their unliving palate. Some types of blood, like fey or vampire blood, affect them almost like alcohol.

### Regions

##### Lairs

Vampires are naturally driven to construct some form of lair, as a protection for their coffin. With their long lives, it is easy for them to accumulate wealth and become the archetypal picture of a decadent vampire living in a grand castle or manor sipping blood from a wine glass. Other vampires may prefer to lean into their predatorial nature, making their lair in a cave, or deciding to haunt a ruin. Their lairs are gloomy reflections of their wretched nature, shrouded in darkness to protect themselves from the sun.

##### Sword Coast

The cities of the Sword Coast have long been the playground of vampires hiding in the shadows, hunting the unlucky at night or manipulating the local politics to their desires. And the bigger the city, the easier for a vampire to hide, or to burn identities and make new ones. The villainous city of Skullport, located in the dungeon of Undermountain below Waterdeep, welcomes undead inhabitants. Some vampires find turbulent and disreputable Luskan a hospitable place for shady dealings and scheming.

##### Thay

Ruled by the tyrannical lich Szass Tam, Thay is an attractive place for certain vampires where they can live openly. So long as they bend the knee, and join the undead army, of course. Thay has the greatest concentration of undead in all Faerun, though most of it are zombies used for menial labor, and necromancy is an accepted practice. Some vampires flock to officer positions in the unliving legions, perhaps drawn by the promise of learning the secrets to lichdom.

### Appearance

**Ashen Complexion:** A vampire is a walking corpse, and very much looks like it. Their skin turns ashen in death, losing its pigment and becoming cold or clammy to the touch. Blood no longer circulates through their bodies, and their veins often turn visible as a sign of their corruption and stagnancy. Sometimes, they'd take great care to conceal their undead nature with the help of makeup and perfumes.

**Red Eyes:** Whatever their eye color in life, their eyes turn an eerie reddish color in death. They take on a predatorial glow, with the sight described as so striking it is unforgettable, by those who survive meeting a vampire. Much like with their skin, the veins in their eyes may become visible or take on a permanent bloodshot look, a symptom of their curse they may try to hide with illusions.

**Fangs: **An effect of their unliving curse, they grow a set of long retractable canines to bite and draw blood from their prey. Their other teeth may turn sharper in their transformation, and their nails turn to glassy claws.

#### Varied Appearances

Vampires look much like how they appeared in life, only in an undead mockery of it. Some may be more feral in appearance, while others attempt to retain a semblance of their mortal life. Fledgeling vampires can guise themselves with illusions, while elder vampires can tap into the gifts of undeath to polymorph their bodies entirely.

### Faith

#### Dead Three

Myrkul, once the god of death and decay, has a legion of undead followers. His faith welcomes the undead, with necromancers common among his worshippers. Bane, the god of tyranny, is the state religion of Thay and often worshipped by Thayan vampires. Many city vampires feuding for control turn to his worship as well. Some vampires who lean into the image of a predator may turn to the worship of Bhaal, the god of murder.

#### Shar

The goddess of loss, darkness, and the night. Her portfolio means many vampires turn to her worship for further power hunting in the dark of night, and she accepts undead amongst her followers. Some vampires, particularly newly turned ones who cling to the trappings of their mortal lives, worship her to deal with their loss as they try to adjust.

#### Kiaransalee

The drow goddess of undeath, a former necromancer queen. Commonly worshipped by drow vampires and necromancers, she drives her worshippers to animate undead and spread the gift of undeath. A spiteful and cruel goddess, she ascended from lichdom and has clung tightly to her divinity since. The Banshee Queen often feuds with Orcus, the Demon Lord of Undeath, seeking to erase all trace of him.

### Coffin Rules

#### Coffin Registration

Coffins will have a registration POI with a DC 20 religion check or DC 25 arcana check attached to them to identify them as a vampire's coffin. Players must either submit a screenshot of a successfully passed DC or have logs of knowledge gained through RP to identify it as a Vampire's coffin.

Coffin POI Format:

- A mundane description describing the coffin's physical appearance (flavor text that serves to identify it in a mundane way)  A DC 20 Religion Check

- A description identifying it as a vampire coffin  A DC25 Arcana Check

- A description identifying it as a vampire coffin.  RULES

Vampires can cast the Locate Object ritual, regardless of their class, on their coffin with a week long cooldown due to their unique bond with the coffin. This has no material cost and can still be countered via spells like Non-Detection.

Coffins may be destroyed and remade: Vampires may only remake their coffin once a month, and only after their current coffin is destroyed to prevent vampires from having multiple failsafe's at any given time.  There are no unique requirements to destroy the coffin.

This material cost for this remake requires:

- 5 Large Component Pouches (the component cost of a resurrection ritual)

- 1 Plank (any)

- 1 Iron Ingot (nails)

Coffins have a soul and therefore count as a person for the purposes of the Teleport Ritual. This means, for example, you may teleport at maximum 4 people + 1 coffin.

In the instance a coffin is stolen by a faction, Vampires may infiltrate the faction for the purposes of destroying/retrieving their coffin without waiting for a DM to guide the infiltration. They must still open a ticket and follow all infiltration rules. Players who steal the coffin must re-register the coffin at the location they place it after theft. Stolen coffins may not be stored in demi-planes. Vampires are not considered "within" the coffin during theft, and cannot be captured this way without explicit consent from the Vampire's player.
