#### Racial Perks

**Darkvision**
This creature can see in the dark.

**Disguise Self Ability**
Channel illusory magic to change your appearance for 20 hours by casting the Disguise Self ritual.

**Bestow Curse Ritual**
This creature gains access to the Bestow Curse ritual.

**Hex**
Bonus Action: Select 1 target within 12 tiles. They take 1d4 points of Necrotic damage at the end of their turn for 3 turns. This DOT can be increased via Upcasting.

**Fluff Spells**
This creature has access to the fluff spells Minor Illusion & Prestidigitation.

**Fey Ancestry**
This creature is immune to sleep effects.

**Natural Focus**
Due to the Hexblood's, inherent magical abilities enhanced through their Living Eldercross, if it is ever removed the Hexblood immediately receives Medium wounds that cannot be healed unless a regeneration ritual is done to regenerate the Eldercross. Until it regrows in Three IRL days from the time of removal, the Hexblood is unable to cast any magic.

## Race Information

### Society

#### The Pact's Shadow

##### Children of Bargains

Hexbloods are the products of some sort of influence from a hag, whether that be a pact gone awry, a deal struck or an interference in the womb is entirely unique to many. Some are born into their strange condition, others remade through magic, but all carry the influence of their benefactor.

##### Solitary but Seeking

Hexbloods rarely gather in large communities. They travel as seekers of purpose, answers, or ways to reclaim their stolen identities. When they do find what little acceptance the world can offer, they cling fiercely to chosen families. Though they are solitary in nature, they are often untamed, wild in spirit, but manipulative and calculating as their hag's influence.

##### Marked Outsiders

Even when raised among other folk, Hexbloods are often regarded with suspicion and even sometimes hatred. Their appearance and innate magic, cause many to treat them as omens of bad luck, spies for the very hags that cursed them so or simply a new hag in waiting.

##### Between Two Worlds

Hexbloods navigate both mortal and fey realms, sometimes acting as intermediaries or as cautionary tales of what happens when one deals too closely with the likes of a hag. They are cursed beings, cursed to walk the divide between fey, and Mortals…but never truly belong to either. Hexbloods can be born or made almost anywhere in Faerun, with some even being born or created in the feywilds themselves.

### Regions

##### Rootless Wanderers

Hexbloods can be found almost anywhere, drifting between mortal towns, lonely wilds, and the edges of the Feywild. They can often be found on the road seeking shelter, or along the edges of fey-touched glades and groves. With their cursed nature, and usually cold welcome by others, they may travel alone seeking what refuge, or friendships they can. Though no doubt slow to trust those that accept them fully, it can come in time.

##### The Feywild

Hexbloods with their innate magic and cursed nature, could have been a simple elf born of the feywild since cursed in a devious pact or deal gone wrong. Hexbloods could also have been entirely born there, altered or touched by a hag whilst still in the womb, causing them to be hexblood-born. Though their notable features, crowns and lurid skin tones may not reveal themselves until later years, they are as cursed as any hexblood…perhaps even more so.

##### The Dessarin Valley

In the Sword Coast region, Hexbloods are often encountered near the High Forest and the Dessarin River. The High Forest hides numerous fey crossings and bargains with hags are a plenty, with tales spun from inn to inn. Many Hexbloods born here claim to have dreamt of its greenery since childhood. Druids and rangers of the region sometimes take in these children, teaching them to harness their gifts.

### Appearance

**Crown Born:** Every Hexblood bears a mark upon their brow horns, antlers, or thorny circlets that grow as if they were part of the body. Some resemble twisted branches or living bone, others seem crystalline, or sprout like briars curling through their hair. These "crowns" are unmistakable and cannot be removed without harm. These crowns often reflect the appearance of the hag or hags which cursed them.

**Witch Touched Flesh:** Their skin often sways from the normal skin tones we see everyday. Lurid shades of grey, green, violet, or corpse-pale hues are common, sometimes resembling patterns of bark, or stone.Hexbloods markings can bear a subtle glow, reflecting their cursed nature, and sometimes, their time in the feywilds. These skin tones often reflect the appearance of the hag or hags which cursed them.

**Eyes of Omens:** Their eyes are one of their most defining features, glimmering with unnatural colors, perhaps bearing too many rings within their iris', or glowing faintly in dim light. Their eyes often reflect the appearance of the hag or hags which cursed them.

#### Varied Appearances

**Hag Touched: **Crooked, angular features, hooked noses, elongated fingers tipped with claw-like nails, and hair that grows thick and unruly.

**Fey Blessed: **Smooth, symmetrical features with an otherworldly allure, iridescent hair that catches the light, and skin possessing vibrant undertones of rose, teal, or gold.

**Covenborn: **Their flesh carries visible remnants of their curse, whether it is dark runic birthmarks across their skin, thorny growths sprouting along the forearms or collarbones, or pupils shaped like crescent moons.

### Faith

#### The Raven Queen, Mistress of Fate

To many Hexbloods, the Raven Queen is the single most important divine figure. She embodies the inevitability of death, the weight of memory, and the severing of bonds that no longer serve. For Hexbloods who wish to be free from their hag makers, she is a beacon of release, one who might cut the cords of destiny that bind them. Her worshippers among Hexbloods often keep small altars with tokens of memory — feathers, trinkets, or fragments of past lives — offered up so that she might take their pain and grant clarity. Though aloof and cold, her distant presence feels more merciful to many Hexbloods than the cruel attentions of their progenitors.

#### Fenmarel Mestarine, the Lone Wolf (Seldarine Pantheon

Fenmarel is the estranged god of the Seldarine, cast apart from his kin for his distrust and solitude. His story resonates deeply with Hexbloods, who so often find themselves outcast from mortal families and societies. To them, Fenmarel is both patron and mirror — a reminder that survival outside the bounds of community is possible, and that estrangement can be its own form of strength. Hexbloods who whisper his name often do so while camping at the edge of forests or wandering empty roads, asking for the vigilance of a hunter and the patience of a wolf that stalks alone.

#### Shar, Mistress of the Night (Faerûnian Pantheon)

Among the Hexbloods most marked by secrecy, curses, or shadow, Shar is a natural figure of dread and devotion. As the goddess of night, loss, and hidden truths, she represents the veils that conceal the bargains which birthed them. Some Hexbloods curse her name, blaming her for the eternal shadow of their existence; others offer her prayers in hope that she will hide their nature, cloaking them in the safety of obscurity. Though rarely a goddess of comfort, Shar is an undeniable presence in the lives of Hexbloods, embodying the silence of forgotten deals and the darkness from which their destiny was forged.
