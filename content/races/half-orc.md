## Race Information

### Society

Though half orcs shed many of the evil tendencies of their full blooded brethren they do share some striking similarities. Greyish skin, jutting tusks, and thick black hair marks them clear and true to their origins. Often quick to rise and rather outspoken half orcs stand for what they believe in and are more than willing to make such beliefs known.

Among human settlements half orcs often find employment in more physical occupations. Though their taller heftier frame makes for an excellent laborer it also allows half orcs to excel in adventuring. Retiring their mallets and chisels for a shortsword and armor often proves a viable escape from a less than welcoming lifestyle.

Living with full blooded orcs proves no easy life for their half blooded kin, often considered smaller and weaker half orcs are often forced to rely on their cooler temper and superior intellect to carve out a life. In truth half orcs were rarely weaker physically, this coupled with a more tolerable visage made them prime traders and allowed a presence in circles that would otherwise not be possible.

Since the rise and fall of the Spellplague most half orcs come not from the union between a proper orc and a human but that of other half orcs. Though many humans pay little heed to this fact the truth of the matter is it allows a far more familial connection for half orcs, a life born not from violence and aggression but love and caring.

### Regions

Though half orcs had no true place to call home their kind spread far and often rose in number to match less uncommon races. Whether travelers proper or the leftover remnants from the Many-Arrows many tribal movements, half orcs often found themselves in places hostile and unwelcoming. Forcing a near cultural distrust of other races half orcs make do where they can, creating safety for themselves where others would find only hardship.

The city of Waterdeep held a sizable population of half orcs serving as guards for the many merchants and traders that traveled to and from the city. Should they seek to make a home of the city the guard could often make use of them on gates and watchtowers. Likewise the city watch could turn a brutish half orc into a right proper constable geared to uphold the law and safety of the city.

Any half orc who thought themselves a craftsman could find purchase in Neverwinter. Producing arms and armor for those stout enough to wield them or perhaps finer odds and ends if they were so inclined. Should production prove too difficult the city and its inhabitants could always benefit from independent gatherers of raw goods, excess iron or herbs rarely sits unwanted and is often paid well for.

### Appearance

**Grey Skinned: ** Though particularly thin blooded half orcs could adopt a skin color far more similar to their human parentage this is not terribly common. The grey or brown coloration of proper orcs tends to prove a dominant trait.

**Tusks:** Jagged teeth protruding forwards like that of a boar, sharp enough to puncture flesh and large enough to be seen from across a room. Some half orcs treat these as a sign of pride, others a mark of shame.

**Powerful Builds: ** Though many are quick to point out the height of half orcs what often goes unspoken is their muscle mass and density. In all dimensions these creatures are larger than your average man.

**Hairy: ** Perhaps a trait derived from most orc's mountainous heritage, their lineage often prove to be fairly hairy beings. Thick coarse hair often black in color made them stand out further from their human kin.

### Faith

#### Tempus

Though half orcs may be removed from their parentage the violence inherent in orcs still curses their blood. This lingering bloodlust often aligned half orcs with gods of violence and destruction, this urge coupled with the mixing of more civil society drives many half orcs towards Tempus. The Lord of Battle teaches combat and destruction as a force of nature to be respected, to solve problems, and to force respect and honor. There is no room for cowardice or underhanded tricks in the temple of the Foehammer.

#### Gruumsh One Eye

Half orcs raised among proper orcs pick up their belief systems. The orcish pantheon is dominated by Gruumsh One Eye, the father and chief of the deific tribe. His way is war and conquest, the taking of all that is rightfully orcish property. Orcs are taught through shamans that Gruumsh watches them all eternally, he never sleeps and never tires and therefore sees all, this often spurs many orcs and half orcs alike into fierce fervor before a battle or challenge they are soon to face.

#### Kelemvor

For those half orcs who do not pay homage to the orcish pantheon Kelemvor may find some degree of favor. The Lord of the Dead taught that death was merely a part of the cycle of all things, an eventuality that would come to all. Many who would frequent combat and adventure could offer prayer to the Judge of the Damned if nothing more than to raise hope that this day would not be their last.
