## Race Information

### Society

#### Reflection of the Elements

##### Familial Connection

Though Genasi could be extremely passionate in a way that might seem violent, the race as a whole had a love for family and friends, to the point that few Genasi marriages ended in divorce. Post-Spellplague, most Genasi families were fairly open due to the fact that Genasi as a whole looked upon another Genasi as a potential relative to the point of referring to those they met as "brother", "sister", or "cousin". In the infrequent Genasi society, family played an important role, likely in part because Genasi found it difficult to form new friendships and bonds.

##### Solitary yet proud

Genasi were exceptionally rare among the planes. They were more common on Toril, however, because of the many portals to other planes found on that world. Even so, until the time after the Spellplague, Genasi were lucky if they ever met another one of their kind. As such, Genasi often lead lonely lives, feeling somewhat defined by their stark differences to most, more common races. Though few in number, Genasi carry themselves with an ever confident stride, whether it is expressed in fiery passion, storm born freedom, unfaltering strength or flowing adaptability depends solely on their heritage. Even with most mortal societies regarding Genasi with a certain caution and unease, they too are mortals, yet too strange to be considered such. The unease from mortal races is not to be confused with the fact that unlike many planetouched races, Genasi generally took pride in their unusual features.

##### Elemental Heritage

The average Genasi's personality mirrored their intrinsic element. For instance, an earth Genasi may take life slowly and cautiously, whereas a fire Genasi may have a "hot temper." Due to their origins in the forces of the Elemental Chaos, Genasi were inherently adaptable. Genasi had a strong ability to adapt, perhaps only superseded by humans in this regard, and embraced change as an inevitability or even something to be relished. Even lawful Genasi embraced this change, and Genasi of all kinds valued the possibilities the future might bring, such as new allies or new opportunities. In many ways, Genasi were the most flexible of races, less stubborn and more willing to accept new ideas for better or worse.

##### Ambitious by Nature

Strong ambition was one of the characteristics shared by all Genasi. Every Genasi person wanted to improve themselves and rise in society, whether it was by pursuing power, making friends, or using other strategies. Though few Genasi were genuinely patient, almost all of them had dreams and aspirations that they would pursue throughout their lives. Their drive for these goals were so strong in fact, that they would continue to strive for them, even long after humans or other races alike would have given up. They were steadfast believers in needing to make a name for themselves, and leave a mark as was fate's design.

### Regions

##### The Chaotic Elementals

Genasi of Faerûn usually lived in isolation until the Spellplague. When Genasi gathered together in great numbers, such as in Calimshan or Akanûl in the time after the Spellplague, their social structures were almost certain to be chaotic and unstable as is their elemental heritage. In Faerûn, they could be found in isolated wildlands or in major cities, such as Waterdeep, where their strange traits would not arouse significant suspicion.

##### Chaotic Elementals of the Planes

Though rare, some Genasi can hail directly from their associated elemental planes. Born among the vast open skies of Air, The City of Brass's volcanic rivers, the City of Glasses unbreakable dome, or the deep cavernous trenches of the very Earth itself. Such individuals often come to Faerun through Planar rifts, deals with patrons or great strides in magic. They tend to view the mortal worlds as…fragile, and sometimes can struggle to adapt to the differences of life between their home and the mortal world, sometimes seeking to wreak storms, flames, tides and stone upon the lands they now walk.

##### Chaotic Elementals of the Sword Coast

Scattered between domains, Genasi walk in the bustling cities and frontiers along the sword coast. In thriving centres such as Waterdeep, Baldur's gate and even Neverwinter, they may find trades as mercenaries, mages or even simple jobs such as bartending, or opening a shop. The differing elements of Genasi can be found widespread along the Sword Coast, from Fire Genasi in the sword mountains, Air Genasi along the windy cliffs near the sea of swords, Earth Genasi in the mines of Amn, and finally, Water Genasi dwelling in the coastal rivers and lakes aplenty. Though by no means a race people may come across everyday, the Sword Coast's openness makes it one of the most welcoming places for things such as Genasi to settle roots.

### Appearance

##### Elemental Touched

Every Genasi carries the essence of their element visibly upon their bodies, crackling flames in their hair, glowing eyes like gemstones or skin hued in strange tones, often aligned with their current elemental heritage. No two Genasi were alike in appearance. Some could pass as normal humans, bearing the marks of their human ethnicity, for others, their elemental nature was impossible to hide. Their heritage was nearly always evident in one or two physical traits, such as skin or hair color or an elemental aura.

##### Not so different

Genasi were about the same size and stature as normal humans, but their bodies displayed signs of greater physical strength, and other subtle indications of their heritage. This greater strength made them hardier than mere humans however, allowing them to endure more than the average person. Thriving in conditions hard for others, but akin to them, like Fire Genasi resisting flames, or Water Genasi moving easily through the depths of the waters.

##### Inherent Energy (Szuldar)

Regardless of their elemental manifestation, the body of each such Genasi was etched with strange lines of energy, called szuldar in Primordial, that glowed in a color associated with the element that the Genasi was currently manifesting. These lines appeared in a pattern that was passed along family lines, sometimes extending into small communities as well. Though the patterns could be similar between relatives, the specific configurations were unique to each individual and served much the same purpose that fingerprints did amongst humans. The pattern of these lines remained largely unchanged. Many Genasi, especially adventurers, displayed these lines with pride.

#### Varied Appearances

##### Air Genasi

Skin tones can range from pale blue to sky-grey, hair can also follow these ranges, usually wild and free flowing in the wind. Eyes that glow with silver or blue.

##### Earth Genasi

Dark stony skin, sometimes with veins of glowing minerals throughout their form, heavy, steadfast frames and eyes like obsidian or the minerals of the earth.

##### Fire Genasi

Reddish or coal black skin tones, hair that flickers or glows like the very flames they embody, and eyes that glow like burning embers.

##### Water Genasi

Skin in hues of turquoise, sea green or deep blues, hair that looks constantly wet, like it's drifting through open water, and eyes like pools of the water, or liquid silver.

### Faith

#### Akadi, The Queen of the Air

As the goddess of elemental air, Akadi is likely to resonate with Air Genasi. Known as The Lady of Air Lady of the Winds or the Queen of Air. She embodies the ever-changing, dynamic, and unpredictable nature of the air and the sky. As an immortal being of freedom and travel, the Queen of Air instructed her followers to move as much as possible from place to place and from activity to activity values that align with the typical Air Genasi personality. Her followers among the Genasi often live as wanderers, sailors, or messengers, whispering prayers to her before embarking on journeys or when caught in storms.

#### Grumbar, King of Earth

The Elemental Lord of Earth, Grumbar is a primary deity for those who revere the elemental power of earth. He symbolizes solidity, stability, and unyielding strength. Known as Earthlord, Gnarly One, King of the Land Below the Roots, Boss of Earth and Boss of the Earth. Grumbar was a quiet and resolute being, who resisted change more so than other divine powers. He bore the weight of secret knowledge and protected the great riches that were found within the earth. To Earth Genasi, Grumbar is the unmoving, and ever enduring voice of absolute reason, with the strength of mountains and the patience of stone. Genasi worship of Gumbar often reflects resilience, tradition and stability.

#### Kossuth, The Lord of Flames

is a primary deity for those who revere the elemental power of fire. He symbolizes the purifying and destructive aspects of fire. Known as The Firelord, Lord of Flames, The Tyrant-King and the Tyrant Among Fire. Kossuth embodies purification through fire and elemental supremacy. To Fire Genasi, Kossuth represents the restless spark of life, passion, destruction and rebirth through the very flames he inherits. Some see him as the patron of willpower and purity, believing fire shall burn away all weakness, given enough time. There were others that feared his cruel indifference, seeing him as a reminder of the consuming danger fire has. Among Fire Genasi, devotion to Kossuth is often fiery and uncompromising.

#### Istishia, Lord of Water

The elemental deity of water, Istishia represents all forms of water, including seas, rivers, and rain. He symbolizes the life-giving, nurturing, and destructive aspects of water. Known as The Water Lord, King of the Water and The God-King of Undines. While other powers governed oceans, pools, streams, storms, or simply those that had business with the water, Istishia was far more abstract. The Water Lord was mutable and dynamic, representing constant change with an essential constant at its core, the god not of any specific body or state of water, but the water itself. To Water Genasi, Istishia was the formless god of rivers, seas and currents. He embodies the world's constant state of change, and the endless waves of the sea. Genasi that follow him value adaptability, patience and the strength of the sea they embody. To them, of course water may yield, but it always endures. Many Genasi sailors, priests and travellers call upon istishia to calm storms or to guide them across the treacherous depths of the seas.
