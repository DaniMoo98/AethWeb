## Race Information

### Society

##### Ambitious Socialites

Yuan-Ti purebloods exist at the bottom of Yuan-Ti society, the lowest born and least blessed by Sseth must work harder in more mundane ways to prove their worth. This aspiration for status and position often drives purebloods to extreme religious dedication or taking steps to promote their clan or temple's standing.

##### Espionage

Every pureblood outside their hatching grounds is a spy, their intent and goals though always self serving are geared towards the collecting and discreet distribution of information. Once free of the domineering serpentine cult their goals may broaden but their secretive nature tends to remain.

##### Distaste for the Unclean

Yuan-Yi of all walks find the scent of humans detestable, so much so that rulers of serpentine society may enforce perfume baths to assuage the stench. Despite their odor humans still find themselves as a suitable meal and their blood a sufficient drink for the carnivorous Yuan-Ti.

##### Outcasts

To little surprise some intelligent or particularly self interested Yuan-Ti seek to flee the dangerous clutches of their homes. Labeled outcasts, these purebloods often try to create distance from their people in pursuit of safety, often leading to seclusion or alliances with unsuspecting races.

### Regions

Stone temples laden with vines and plant growth aplenty dot deep forests and long forgotten vales, such places provide dark and damp spot perfect for the serpentine Yuan-Ti. While they are not a purely detached folk they do enjoy their seclusion and secrecy when viable, often leading to lives spent imitating humanoids when needed and reveling in serpentine ritual elsewhere. Such a preference leaves few suitable dwellings within city limits.

##### Ss'tck'al

Just east of the High Moor lies the Serpent Hills, a large swath of land inhabited on the surface by innumerable snakes, dragons, Yuan-Ti, and other serpentine demi-races. Far below the hills deep within the caverns of the underdark rests the ruins of Ss'tck'al, a proper though time worn haven for the Yuan-Ti that call it home.

##### Snake Friendly Architecture

When Yaun-Ti take the time to carve out their place in the world they do so with their kin in mind. Their architecture is distinct and capable of being used by all the variants of Yuan-Ti often at the detriment to your average humanoid's mobility. Stairs are replaced by ramps and pillars jutting through holes in the floor, openings allowing sunlight to filter onto warming rooms, and likewise tents offering shade and reprieve from the heat.

### Appearance

**Human Facade:** Within Yuan-Ti society it is the prime directive of a pureblood to sneak, deceive, and ultimately infiltrate a humanoid settlement. To this end many purebloods seek to maintain a convincing human guise, something their biology already aids in greatly, or learning magic that would enable such trickery.

**Eyes:** Needless to say variety is present though the vast majority of purebloods have decidedly reptilian eyes. Many range from human sized iris to proper fully reptilian eyes, given their inherently magical ancestry a Yuan'Ti's eyes can be a broad range of colors.

**Scales:** Though it may be hard to tell from a distance every Yuan-Ti is scaled to some degree. Purebloods from a distance may very well appear to be fully human but upon inspection from a few feet away their scales may peek through clothing or jewelry. Despite their serpentine nature pureblood scales only appear in symmetrical patches across the body.

### Faith

#### Sseth

From an outside perspective Sseth is a cruel and ruthless god, often seen as wholly uncaring for his chosen cultist so long as they venerate him and him alone. Bloody sacrifice, strange rituals, and vile dedication are all committed in Sseth's name by Yuan-Ti who remain loyal to the faith.

#### Dendar

The night serpent is an impossibly old being with a decidedly malicious intent, consuming nightmares and amassing power for the inevitable end of the world. Dendar's followers would often kidnap folks and poison them with sleeping potions, hoping to further feed their god's appetite.

#### Demon Hosts

Though rare, some Yuan-Ti may find it worthwhile to venerate demon lords over their own similarly chaotic evil gods. Baphomet, Graz'zt, and Fraz-Urb'luu are easy choices for their dominion over serpent kind, promise of rich decadence, and a devotion to secrecy and deception respectively.
