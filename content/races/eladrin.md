## Race Information

### Society

#### The Lords of the Seasons

When the elves began migrating to the Material Plane circa -30,000 DR from the Feywilds, those who chose to stay were among the ones who became the eladrin. These sylvan elves are proud of their deep link to the first elves, often lending to a haughty attitude when around other elven races. Some take a kinder view, almost a sort of pity that the elven races of the Material Planes have not experienced the same closeness to the glory of their shared elder ancestors. This haughty attitude, however, makes them very susceptible to flattery.

The eladrin have been engaged in war with the fomorians since the curse befell the race of giants. Once, the races lived in harmony together through a shared love of fey magic, until the fomorians fell to greed and used their magic for naught but malice, bringing upon the cruel fate that transformed the giants into monsters. Driven into the Feydark by the eladrin, the fomorians regularly besiege their cities, blaming the elves with an eternal enmity.

Their architecture is centered on natural splendor, such as glimmering waterfalls or towering trees that dominate the landscape. The style blends with the natural surroundings to the point of almost seeming to grow out of it, and reflect the pinnacle of elven architecture, with soaring towers and delicately arched bridges and ivy or golden filigree decorating every building. They are proud of their ancient culture and believe their architectural style is the closest to the first elves.

The eladrin are the most fickle of all the elven races, ruled by emotion and wild impulse, a result of their fey natures. The season they are attuned to reflects their emotional state. Spring is the season of joy and mischief, the desire for play. Summer is linked to anger, vindictiveness, aggression, and a need for justice. Autumn is for reflection, contemplation, compassion, and goodwill. And winter bleeds sorrow and melancholy, a bitter and gloomy season.

### Regions

##### The Sylvan Elves

The eladrin primarily live in their home plane of the Feywilds, with how thoroughly they have made it their home. With their capricious nature, they sometimes leave the Feywilds to adventure across the Material Plane, for a grand variety of reasons. Some quest for glory to prove themselves to the Courts, some elect to aid the other elven races as guides with their fey magics. Others guard fey crossings or fey who dwell on the Material Plane, or find themselves simply struck by wanderlust.

##### Feywilds

The eladrin have a number of grand cities dotting Faerie, and can be found in small pockets near everywhere in the fey plane, or serving archfey in their Domains of Delight. The city of Astrazalian boasts twining crystal spires upon hills that seem carved out of emerald, its residents claiming the reputation of the grandest city in the entire plane, perhaps even all the planes. In the eternally autumn city of Mithrendain, time passes by so slowly that there are no timepieces, nor even a sense of early or late, only basking in the eternal moment. Shinaelastra is a city with a most grim fate, beset upon by the armies of the fomorian queen Connomae every night, their lives shaped by this constant warfare but their fierce fighting spirits remain unbroken and their halls boast grand revels and feasts after every victory.

##### Moonshae Isles

In 1375 DR, known also as the Year of Risen Elfkin, the High Lady Ordalf teleported the eladrin city of Karador into the Material Plane. It landed on the island Gwynneth in the Moonshae Isles, now called Sarifal by the new fey residents. A gleaming city of crystal in the middle of the Lake Myrloch, it is protected by a mythal and its magically powerful ruling family. The transplanted city has long looked down upon humans and any race that does not have ties to the fey, but have recently lifted restrictions on visitors. Some eladrin have found the Queen a cruel ruler and settled elsewhere in Moonshae, the isles' strong ties of the Feywild making them feel at home and yet satisfying their whimsical desires for adventure.

### Appearance

**Elven Appearance:** The eladrin appear most similar to sun elves, with lithe and light builds, with even the strongest among them showing little muscularity. Their facial features are slender and sharp, along with a perpetual gracefulness to their movement. Eladrin typically have longer ears than other elven races, an influence of their fey nature.

**Seasonal Emotions:** An eladrin's appearance reflects the season they are currently attuned to. Some eladrins shift with the changing of seasons in real-time. Some change with each passing mood, some only with powerful emotions. Some are so entrenched in a mood they have not changed in years. Spring boasts bright greens, mild yellows, and earthy browns. Summer comes with vibrant golds, coppers, and soft oranges. Autumn is associated with deep oranges, woody browns, and soft golds. Winter tells of melancholic blues, sharp whites, and piercing black.

**Influence of Magic:** The eladrin are a race adept with magic, the nature of the fey plane affecting their forms. They typically have a glow to their eyes, and for eladrin who use magic daily, they often have fey runes or markings that glow etched into their skin.

#### Varied Appearances

Some eladrin have eyes that glow all one color, pupil and sclera and iris. Some have two-toned eyes, with colored sclera. Eladrin eyes, hair, and skin all change color with their attuned season, often vibrant shades reflecting their fey ties.

### Faith

#### Corellon

The eladrin have a close connection to Corellon, their ancestry so tied to the first elves. However, most feel little affinity for Arvandor and do not desire to rejoin Corellon like the other elven races, though some eladrin have proven themselves the exception to this.. They view Corellon as the pinnacle of elvenhood and strive to emulate him, a subtle devotion woven into the fabric of their culture.

#### Erevan Ilesere

An exarch of Corellon, the elven deity of tricksters and mischief is also known as the Jack of the Seelie Court or the Fey Jester. He has proven fiercely protective of the sylvan races, with many fey participating in his rituals and ceremonies. The fickle nature of the eladrin means they often see themselves in the Trickster.

#### Solonor Thelandira

The elven god of hunting, archery, and survival, he is often known as Keen-Eye. He watches over the borders between wilderness and civilization, and thus is often the patron of those who guard fey crossings. Many eladrin who prefer the wilds to the cities also favor worshipping him.
