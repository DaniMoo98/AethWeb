**Darkvision**
This creature can see in the dark.

This creature gains access to the Create or Destroy Water and Shape Water spells.

**Aquatic**
Gain access to the following benefits while the Swimming status is active:

- 15% vulnerability to Lightning damage, stacking with other sources of vulnerability

- 50% resistance to Fire damage, does not stack with other sources of Fire resistance

- +1 Initiative while swimming

- +1 to tracking rolls when tracking through major waterways

- No Attack penalty while swimming

- No reduction to Somatic Components for spellcasting

- No Movement penalty while swimming, regardless of armor class

## Race Information

### Society

#### The Protectors of the Deep

The triton race was created by their patron deity in the Elemental Plane of Water, and soon dedicated themselves to battling the forces of aquatic evil, eventually quarantining their foes to the Darkened Depths of the Plane of Water. After centuries of quiet, expeditions revealed their foes had escaped to the Material Plane. Duty drove many tritons to follow them, blaming themselves for allowing their enemies to escape and endanger the Elemental Plane.

The sahuagin are the sworn enemies of their race, but tritons also hunt krakens, morkoths, aquatic undead, kuo-toa, aboleths, anguiliians, eyes of the deep, ixixachitl, koalinths, and merrow. They believe defending the world from these evils is their honorable duty, and will give their lives in the effort if it means a victory over their ancient foes. Sometimes they will come to land in search of foes hiding on the surface, or hunting the agents of krakens and aboleths.

Tritons often bear a haughty attitude, believing they should be respected for their long protection of the world. They can be arrogant and quick to boast of triton victories over the dreadful underwater threats. Many tritons are seen as somber or grim, with their single-minded focus . Still, they are nearly all benevolent and fiercely compassionate, and some come to land out of curiosity regarding the surface people they protect.

Despite sharing the seas with them, many aquatic elves have never seen a triton due to the race's isolation and triton settlements commonly being on the seafloor while sea elven settlements are commonly in the shallows. They have fought the sea elven empire before, though have allied Myth Nantar more recently. Tritons count shalarins and merfolk as close allies, and often ride hippocampi into battle. They have little familiarity with surface people, finding their ways confusing, but sometimes they will seek out surface people to learn how they fight, to expand triton martial knowledge.

### Regions

##### Aquatic Folk

Their settlements are formed around areas they expect to find their enemies, such as deep sea trenches or portals to the Plane of Water. Primarily, they live in the Triton Protectorates in Seros. They have smaller settlements across most major bodies of water, including the Sea of Swords, the Trackless Sea, and the Great Sea. All structures within their settlements were made of either coral or stone, all built according to strict geometric layouts. Some tritons also reside in Myth Nantar.

##### Seros

The majority of tritons live in the three patriarchal kingdoms at the bottom of the Sea of Fallen Stars, Ahlorsath, Pumanath, and Vuuvaxath. Commonly known as the 'Triton Protectorates' to outsiders, the kingdoms are centered around the massive Trench of Lopok, guarding the imprisoned malevolent entities within that are only known as 'Those Who Sleep Below'. Each is an independent realm, made up of a capital city, ringed by eight core trade cities, which in turn were ringed by twelve farming towns. Finally, twenty-four outposts surrounded the towns, forming a defensive perimeter, an orderliness that reflects their regimented lives.

##### Plane of Water

Many tritons still live on the Elemental Plane of Water, where they may serve as bodyguards or courtiers of marids, genies native to the Plane. Some visit or have the honor of living at and working in the Citadel of Ten Thousand Pearls, the home of Kalbari al-Durrat al-Amwaj ibn Jari, the Mother of Foam and Great Padisha of Marids. Some tritons live in the City of Glass, the Plane of Water's hub of trade. Others venture into the little known reaches of the plane, hunting for elemental evils hiding in the endless ocean.

### Appearance

##### Life Underwater

Triton physiology is well-suited to life underwater, with webbed ears, hands, and feet. Their skin is commonly an ocean-blue tone, but other colors are possible, like silver and light green. They are notably shorter than humans, rarely more than five feet tall. They have nictitating membranes over their eyes, though otherwise triton eyes appear much like humans, in shades of striking blue or green. In darkness or deep water, their pupils will expand, sometimes making the entire eye look black. Their skin has a permanent sheen to it.

##### Fins

A notable feature of tritons are the dorsal fins on their legs, coming in various colors but usually of a light shade. Some have smaller fins on their forearms. These help keep their balance when underwater, and aids in swimming faster and smoother. When on land, they take great care to keep them moisturized.

##### Hair

Triton hair tended to be dark green, reminiscent of a mane of kelp, or a deep blue akin to the colors of the ocean. Men typically keep it braided so it stays out of their eyes, though women will more often let it fly free. When expecting combat, they will often tie it into a tight ponytail. Their hair is often decorated with coral or bits of metal, but this has a practical purpose of being an improvised weapon if they are disarmed.

##### Varied Appearances

Some tritons appear more aquatic than others, with scales along their body, particularly their lower legs. Others have flippers to their feet or more webbing to their ears than others. Some appear more humanoid, with pearly skin and less webbing to their ears, though these are often treated as social outcasts.

### Faith

#### Persana

The tritons revere Persana as the creator of their race, depicting him as a tall and well-muscled triton carrying a pink and green conch shell. To the Merfolk, he goes by the name Eadro. Triton priests play a significant role in their culture, well-regarded as wise leaders, and they would readily aid those of other faiths in the name of their god. A god of craftsmanship and guardianship, he encourages his followers to build grand cities and to pursue their racial duty of hunting elemental evils.

#### Istishia

The primordial deity of elemental water, he is especially well-regarded by tritons who still live on the Elemental Plane, or triton druids revering him as the embodiment of water itself. Some tritons who lean into the destructive power of water also worship him, as well as tritons who live among sea elves or water genasi that worship Istishia. Some outcasts find his dispassionate and eternally neutral nature appealing.

#### Umberlee

A rare few tritons turn from their noble responsibility of hunting their ancient foes, sometimes collapsing under the stress of duty or becoming manipulated by those very enemies into the embrace of evil. These tritons may end up worshipping Umberlee, beseeching the Bitch Queen of the Sea for her power or aiding her kraken and sahuagin worshippers against their own kin. These tritons find relief from the strict lives of their culture in the chaotic tumult of the Wavemother's church.
