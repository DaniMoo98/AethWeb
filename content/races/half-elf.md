## Race Information

### Society

#### Of Two Worlds

Growing up, Half-Elves often felt isolated and alone - no matter who they were raised by. If they grew up in an elven community, the feeling oftentimes was more intense though, as in big cities with more diversity - such as Baldurs Gate or Waterdeep.. Compared to Elves, they reached Maturity rather fast - by age 20. Their Childhood was therefore more akin to their human parent or relatives.

Half-Elves do not have any distinct society of their own - always feeling caught somewhere in between. They usually adapt to the society they were raised in. They may feel out of place among elves due to their shorter lifespan and worldviews, or may feel out of place among humans because of their longer lifespan and different temperament. Oftentimes, they have a 'foot in both worlds' but do not truly belong to either. They feel a special kinship to other half-elves or half-races that can relate to this feeling.

Renowned for their adaptability, Half-Elves take up various occupations and wander many different paths in life. They often act as diplomats, building relations between human and elven society, as they can appreciate the beauty and wisdom of the elves but can also relate to the ambition and drive of humans. As Half-Elves combine the elven perceptiveness and human passion within them they often exhibit a confidence and strength of character that is not displayed by either of their parents. They're curious and inventive, free-spirited and chaotic and value liberty highly. Sometimes, these qualities will make them unreliable and unpredictable.

Half-Elves present an innate Wanderlust. It is seldom that they stay in one town or region for too long. Despite that - or perhaps because of it - they do enjoy being around others. The more diverse the company, the better! They also have a habit of forming large networks of contacts. Even if they leave a place for a while, upon their return they will gladly get in touch with their former connections once more.

### Regions

##### A Place to Belong

Half-Elves have no homeland of their own. They can be found everywhere where humans and elves come in contact. There are few nations that have a high half-elven population - such as Luruar or the Dalelands. In places where elves and humans live in harmony instead of opposition, their population is usually bigger than elsewhere.

##### Among the Elves of the High Forest

The elves of the high forest can be considered a remnant of the old elven kingdoms. The region had a turbulent past, with many elves leaving during the great Retreat. However, in 1372 wood elves from Evermeet migrated back to found a new kingdom, Earlann, facing plenty of troubles due to the population of orcs, Fey'Ri and Goblins. To this day, their efforts still continue. Half-Elves in this region are rare and grew up amongst a Woodelven Culture. The few humans that dared to venture through the High Forest were rangers or druids. Half-Elves born here live close to nature and danger and do not have much experience with the big diverse cities of Faerun.

##### Among the Humans of the Sword-Coast

The Sword-Coast lies on the western coast of Faerun. Monsters roam through the expansive wilderness, with independent cities scattered between. It is a place that attracts many adventurers. Some of the known cities may be Beregost, Daggerford and Baldurs Gate - influential merchant cities with a mostly human population. Half-Elves who originate from here were likely raised by their human parents - or at least, in an environment that was largely shaped by human culture and religion. They may become merchants, adventurers, or are the sons and daughters of a noble house. It is in these cities where they can strive and rely on their social skills best.

### Appearance

**Neither, nor: **Half-Elves look like humans to the elves - and like elves to humans. Their appearance is neither as slender as the one of the elves, nor as robust as that of their human lineage. Their ears commonly were the size of a human ear, but with a pointed tip.

**Elven Eyes: **They often inherit the eyes of their elven parent - but skin tones and hair colours also showed their elven heritage. Skin tones may have a metallic hue and hair colours may be those of an elven parent as well.

**Human Features:** One distinct feature setting male half-elves apart from elves was the fact that they were able to grow a beard. Many of them embraced this to set themselves apart from their elven cousins.

#### Varied Appearances

**Moon-elven Heritage:** Half-Elves with Moon-elven heritage may have a pale, blueish tint around their ears and chin, even if their skin tone is otherwise more of a human colour. Their hair can be that of their human lineage, or a silvery-white of their elven ancestors.

**Sun-elven Heritage: ** Those of a Sun-elven heritage may have a skin colour reminiscent of bronze. Like any half-elf, their hair could be a colour of their human lineage, or the golden colour of their sun-elven ancestry.

### Faith

#### Corellon, The Protector

The Creator of Elves and the Leader of the Elven Pantheon, Corellon has a love for magic, music, arts, crafts and poetry - in short, he appreciates creation itself. However, he also stands as the protector of the Elves and in that aspect, is their God of Warfare. Like the Elves he is prideful, bordering on arrogance - and yet he still enjoys to learn, even from mortals. He is not only allied with the Seldarine, but also with the gods of other Pantheons - mainly those whose domains are nature and magic, as he is concerned about human expansion. His enemies are the Dark Seldarine, the gods of the Drow, as well as Gruumsh - who Corellon is said to have cut out an eye from.

#### Sune, Lady of Love

Her gift is what has led to the creation of Half-Elves: Love. She is the goddess of beauty and love in all their many forms. While this includes the darker aspects of love such as obsession, Sune is a benevolent goddess who abhorred the destruction of beauty. She may be vain, but tender, and her followers hold a special place in her heart. It is them who she loves the most. It is hard to stay angry at her - which is perhaps the reason she has mostly allies and only a few she considers enemies. Among those she hates are the gods of fury - because they destroy beautiful things.

#### Silvanus, The Oak Father

The Farunian deity of nature - wild and unpredictable just like his domain. Silvanus is very concerned with nature itself, the beauty as well as the savagery. He is defending it fiercely and has no care for a balance with civilisation or the machinations of mortals and other deities alike. Despite that, he has a few allies - deities that care for animals and nature - such as Mielliki, Lathander and Chauntea. The Gods of Fury and Malar count to his enemies. The Oak Father is worshipped by clerics and druids alike and Half-Elves growing up in the Wilderness, raised by druids and rangers, may naturally find themselves among his worshippers.
